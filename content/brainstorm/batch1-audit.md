# Batch 1 Lyric Audit

Applying principles from lyric study (Task 1), songwriting principles (Task 2), and Suno tips (Task 3) to each batch 1 song. For each song: best lines, worst lines, and 2–3 rewrites.

---

## 1. Product-Market Fit (You'll Know)

**Best lines:**
- "Forty percent very disappointed" — specific Rahul Vohra metric that doubles as an emotional gut-punch. Specificity principle nailed.
- "It's the pull you feel from the other side" — shows, doesn't tell. Physical sensation over definition.

**Worst lines:**
- "A hundred answers, none of them precise" — generic, tells instead of shows. What does imprecision look like?
- "Segmented down to lovers, jumped to thirty-three / Three more quarters grinding, hit it — fifty-eight was free" — 14+ syllables, stuffed, will cause Suno mumbling.
- "The metric that I need, nobody knows" — vague, blog-post tone.

**Rewrites:**
1. "A hundred answers, none of them precise" → "A hundred answers, all of them polite" (specificity — politeness is the killer; people are nice instead of honest)
2. "Segmented down to lovers, jumped to thirty-three / Three more quarters grinding, hit it — fifty-eight was free" → "Cut the list to lovers, thirty-three / Nine more months of grinding — fifty-eight came free" (under 12 syllables each, clearer rhythm)
3. "The metric that I need, nobody knows" → "The number that would let me sleep, who knows?" (personal, shows the stakes)

**Chorus note:** Repeated word-for-word. Consider changing "If you have to ask — you don't have it yet" to something evolved on the second pass, e.g., "If you have to ask — keep building, not yet."

## 2. Ship It

**Best lines:**
- "But nothing's real until it's on the outside" — clean, quotable, universal.
- "Honest thing you've ever made — so let it go" — emotional truth in the context of shipping.

**Worst lines:**
- "Afraid the world might see the version that's the most / Honest thing you've ever made — so let it go" — awkward enjambment across lines; "the most" dangles.
- "Speed is not reckless, speed is how you show you care" — thesis statement, not a lyric. Tells directly.
- "Ramp hit a hundred million in two years / Five PMs, fifty engineers — no fears" — "no fears" is a filler rhyme; the line is a press release.
- "The best PMs ship — the rest make decks for free" — tries too hard to be quotable. Every line is a bumper sticker.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Speed is not reckless, speed is how you show you care" → "The fastest hands are gentle — speed is care" (shows through metaphor, singable vowels)
2. "Ramp hit a hundred million in two years / Five PMs, fifty engineers — no fears" → "A hundred million, two years on the clock / Five PMs in a room that wouldn't stop" (drops filler rhyme, more visual)
3. "The best PMs ship — the rest make decks for free" → "The builders ship — the rest just rearrange the slides" (less bumper-sticker, more image)

## 3. The Maze

**Best lines:**
- "But tarpits shine the hardest in the light" — beautiful image, original, earns the metaphor.
- "You're not lost, you're in the maze" — strong hook, reframes fear as process.

**Worst lines:**
- "Gleaming on the surface, sinking for years" — good image but forced to rhyme with "ideas" in the previous line. Sounds stretched.
- "One in three will pivot — B2B / One in five for consumer, that's the fee" — data dump. "That's the fee" is a filler phrase.
- Bridge is weak: "gleaming, hearty" is a forced rhyme, and "always hazy" is a cliché. The bridge restates the chorus instead of adding a new angle.

**Rewrites:**
1. "One in three will pivot — B2B / One in five for consumer, that's the fee" → "One in three will turn the wheel around / One in five for consumer — hold your ground" (keeps data, adds grit)
2. Bridge rewrite: "If your friends all nod at dinner, run / The easy pitch hides the hardest one / The real ideas make you sound insane / The real path always starts in rain" (new imagery, advances the story)
3. "Gleaming on the surface, sinking for years" → "Shining on the surface, pulling you down" (avoids forced rhyme with "ideas," open vowels)

## 4. Vibe Coding

**Best lines:**
- "Don't read the code, just feel the flow" — perfect hook, captures the ethos instantly.
- "Prompt and pray and iterate and test" — rhythmic, fun, honest about the process.

**Worst lines:**
- "Less than a hundred people, six billion — hear" — garbled stat-stuffing. "Hear" is a meaningless filler word.
- "Five hundred builders wrote to Lenny's thread" — insider reference that won't land outside the community.
- "Democratized the making, broke the gate" — "democratized" is 6 syllables of jargon. "Broke the gate" is cliché.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Less than a hundred people, six billion — hear" → "Ninety people, six billion on the line" (cleaner, singable)
2. "Democratized the making, broke the gate" → "Handed everyone the keys and walked away" (visual, conversational, 12 syllables)
3. "Five hundred builders wrote to Lenny's thread" → "Five hundred strangers shipped before they read" (keeps the number, makes it about the universal action)

## 5. The IC Track

**Best lines:**
- "At two AM, which version of you / Is still awake because they want to be?" — powerful question, specific, emotional.
- "I traded craft for meetings before dawn" — shows the loss concretely.

**Worst lines:**
- "Management is not a promotion / It's just a different road" — thesis statement, not a lyric. Reads like a LinkedIn post.
- "Senior ICs pulling one-eighty-seven stacks / Out-earning managers — the data's clear" — jargon-heavy, stat dump. "One-eighty-seven stacks" means nothing to most listeners.
- "She felt her skin / Come alive with purpose, not with power" — overwrought, the enjambment on "skin" creates an awkward pause.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Management is not a promotion / It's just a different road" → "They called it climbing — I called it a door / Into a room where I don't build anymore" (shows the feeling, specific image)
2. "Senior ICs pulling one-eighty-seven stacks / Out-earning managers — the data's clear" → "She earns more than her boss and loves her days / The title shrank, the joy filled up the space" (personal story > stats)
3. "She felt her skin / Come alive with purpose, not with power" → "She touched the keyboard and remembered why / She used to stay up building until five" (concrete action, callback to 2AM motif)

## 6. Talk to Users

**Best lines:**
- "You're reading sheet music but you've never heard the song" — brilliant metaphor, earns its place, quotable.
- "You're not your user" — simple, devastating, works as both hook and truth.

**Worst lines:**
- "Color-coded dashboards, segments, cohort views" — 13 syllables of jargon. Will cause Suno to rush.
- "Continuous discovery, week by week" — "continuous discovery" is corporate speak, 9 closed syllables.
- "The number-one most recommended book / In Lenny's Slack — go take a look" — insider reference + forced rhyme. "Go take a look" is filler.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Color-coded dashboards, segments, cohort views / But somehow every launch still missed the cues" → "We charted every click and built a wall / Of data — but we never made the call" (visual metaphor, singable)
2. "Continuous discovery, week by week" → "One honest question, every single week" (same idea, human language)
3. "The number-one most recommended book / In Lenny's Slack — go take a look" → "The book that changed a thousand teams for good / She wrote the thing we wished we'd understood" (universal, emotional)

## 7. Do Things That Don't Scale

**Best lines:**
- "Go to them, go to them / Physically go to them" — powerful repetition, earns the anthem feel through simplicity.
- "Took the photos, wrote the listing down / Slept on strangers' couches cross the town" — specific, shows the hustle physically.

**Worst lines:**
- "I packed a camera and a carry-on / Knocked on every door from dusk to dawn" — metronomic couplet, feels mechanical. "Dusk to dawn" is cliché.
- "Paloaltodelivery dot com — that's the key" — Suno will butcher this URL. Unspeakable line.
- "That's how you build on solid ground, not sand" — biblical cliché without earning it.

**Rewrites:**
1. "I packed a camera and a carry-on / Knocked on every door from dusk to dawn" → "I packed a camera and a carry-on / Rang the bell and stayed until they yawned" (specific, funny, breaks the mechanical feel)
2. "Paloaltodelivery dot com — that's the key" → "A six-buck flyer and a phone that rang / That's the sound before the rocket sang" (drops the URL, keeps the scrappy energy)
3. "That's how you build on solid ground, not sand" → "That's how you learn a name before a brand" (specific, avoids cliché)

## 8. Imposter

**Best lines:**
- "Everyone at the table / Thinks everyone else belongs more" — universally relatable, clean hook.
- "Your imposter syndrome is lying to you / But I know that doesn't make it quiet" — honest, doesn't pretend a fix exists.

**Worst lines:**
- "Looked around the table, felt the heat" — generic. What kind of heat? What table?
- "Julie Zhuo — thirteen years at the book" — "at the book" is unclear (at Facebook? at the book she wrote?). Confusing reference.
- "Turns out the doubt and growth share the same lane" — cliché metaphor ("same lane"), tells instead of shows.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Looked around the table, felt the heat" → "Counted all the badges, none were mine" (specific object, visual)
2. "Julie Zhuo — thirteen years at the book" → "Thirteen years at Facebook, still she felt / Like someone left the door unlocked by accident" (clear, visual, emotional)
3. "Turns out the doubt and growth share the same lane" → "The doubt got louder every time she grew / That's the part they never tell you's true" (shows the paradox, conversational)

## 9. Burnout

**Best lines:**
- "When did I stop breathing?" — visceral, physical, perfect closing question.
- "Building castles for someone else's sake" — beautiful image that shows the hollow feeling.

**Worst lines:**
- "Burnout isn't working too much / It's working on the wrong things" — thesis statement, not a lyric. Blog post line.
- "Feather, brick, then dump truck — can't deny" — Lenny-specific reference (his burnout metaphor). Means nothing without context.
- "Optimizing for a game you don't want to win / And the scoreboard's bright but the lights are wearing thin" — mixed metaphor (game + scoreboard + lights wearing thin?), overstuffed.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Burnout isn't working too much / It's working on the wrong things" → "I can work all night when it's mine to build / But this — this leaves me standing still" (shows through contrast, doesn't define)
2. "Feather, brick, then dump truck — can't deny" → "First a whisper, then a weight, then a wall / I kept walking like I'd never fall" (universal escalation image, no insider reference)
3. "Optimizing for a game you don't want to win / And the scoreboard's bright but the lights are wearing thin" → "Running up a score I'll never spend / Winning something I don't want to win" (clean, parallel, one metaphor)

## 10. Taste

**Best lines:**
- "Half a pixel off from what I mean" — brilliant specificity, shows taste in action.
- "The data told me no / The user's face told me yes / That's taste" — perfect punchline, earns the whole song.

**Worst lines:**
- "You are lazy, vain, and selfish — that's the lesson" — quotes Belsky but lands as an insult without context. Listeners who don't know the framework will recoil.
- "Taste is saying no to good ideas / And holding out through all the noise and fears" — thesis line again. Tells the definition.
- "Pattern recognition plus the empathy" — corporate jargon dressed as a lyric. 11 syllables of abstraction.

**Rewrites:**
1. "You are lazy, vain, and selfish — that's the lesson" → "They'll give you thirty seconds, maybe less / If it doesn't feel right, they won't confess" (keeps Belsky's idea, makes it about user behavior)
2. "Taste is saying no to good ideas / And holding out through all the noise and fears" → "It's the courage in the no, the wait, the cost / Of killing something good so great's not lost" (shows the pain of taste, emotional)
3. "Pattern recognition plus the empathy" → "She's seen a thousand screens and felt what's off" (personal, concrete, singable)

## 11. Eleven Stars

**Best lines:**
- "The last ten percent is ninety percent of the magic" — Chesky insight as a lyric, works because the math is surprising.
- "A one-star stay is sleeping on the floor / A three-star stay — you get a key and door" — the progression is playful, visual, builds naturally.

**Worst lines:**
- "Elon picks you up in a rocket to the moon" — dated reference, polarizing figure. Will age badly.
- "Brian said start somewhere past absurd / Then work it backwards, trust the pull, not just the word" — compressed and unclear. "Trust the pull, not just the word" is vague.
- "Craft is caring 'bout the things they'll never / Consciously notice, but they'll feel forever" — awkward enjambment on "never/Consciously." The break falls in the wrong place.

**Rewrites:**
1. "Elon picks you up in a rocket to the moon" → "A stranger picks you up and flies you to the moon" (removes polarizing name, keeps the absurdist image)
2. "Brian said start somewhere past absurd / Then work it backwards, trust the pull, not just the word" → "He said imagine past the edge of wild / Then build it backwards like a wide-eyed child" (visual, emotional, drops the name for cleaner flow)
3. "Craft is caring 'bout the things they'll never / Consciously notice, but they'll feel forever" → "Craft is hiding love in things they'll never see / But every time they touch it, they believe" (fixes enjambment, shows instead of explains)

## Cross-Song Patterns

**Recurring problems across all 11 songs:**
1. **Thesis-statement choruses** — 8 of 11 songs have at least one line that reads like a blog post definition ("Burnout isn't...", "Management is not...", "Taste is saying no...")
2. **Identical chorus repetition** — All 11 songs repeat choruses word-for-word. Zero variation.
3. **Stat-stuffing** — 7 songs cram specific numbers into lines that exceed 12 syllables, risking Suno mumbling.
4. **Insider references** — "Feather, brick, then dump truck," "Paloaltodelivery dot com," "Lenny's Slack" — alienate listeners outside the community.
5. **Filler rhymes** — "no fears," "that's the key," "go take a look" — words that exist only to complete a rhyme.

**What batch 1 does well:**
1. **Strong hooks** — "You'll know," "Ship it," "Vibe coding," "Talk to your users" are all memorable, singable.
2. **Emotional range** — album covers joy (PMF), fear (Imposter), exhaustion (Burnout), wonder (Eleven Stars).
3. **Story specificity** — when the songs use real stories (Airbnb couches, Rahul's 22→58 journey), they work.
4. **Best single line across all songs:** "You're reading sheet music but you've never heard the song" (Talk to Users) — metaphor does ALL the work.
