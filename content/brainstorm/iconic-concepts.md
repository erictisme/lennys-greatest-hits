# Iconic Concepts — Song Brainstorm

*Note: Lenny MCP was unavailable this session. Concepts sourced from deep knowledge of Lenny's archive (638 pieces: 349 newsletters, 289 podcasts). Verify quotes against archive when MCP is available.*

## 1. "Do Things That Don't Scale"
- **Iconic concept**: Paul Graham's essay, but Lenny made it REAL with Airbnb/DoorDash/Stripe founding stories. Brian Chesky going door-to-door photographing apartments. The Collison brothers manually installing Stripe for users.
- **Guest/Episode**: Brian Chesky (multiple appearances), DoorDash founding stories, Paul Graham references throughout
- **Quotable lines**: "Go to your users. Physically go to them." / "The things that got us our first 1,000 users would never get us to a million" / "Every great company started with something that didn't scale"
- **Why meme-worthy**: This is THE foundational startup gospel. Every founder has heard it, few actually do it.
- **Genre**: Indie folk, acoustic — like a campfire origin story
- **Album**: Founders

## 2. "Product-Market Fit (You'll Know)"
- **Iconic concept**: Lenny's most-referenced framework. The idea that PMF isn't gradual — it hits you like a truck. Users pulling the product out of your hands.
- **Guest/Episode**: Rahul Vohra (Superhuman), Marc Andreessen quote, multiple Lenny newsletters on PMF surveys
- **Quotable lines**: "If you have to ask, you don't have it" / "40% would be very disappointed" / "It feels like the product is being pulled out of your hands"
- **Why meme-worthy**: Every founder obsessively asks "do we have PMF?" — this captures the anxiety AND the answer.
- **Genre**: Building synth-pop — the tension resolving into euphoria
- **Album**: Founders

## 3. "The Maze"
- **Iconic concept**: Dalton Caldwell's "tarpit ideas" — ideas that SEEM good but trap founders for years. The YC concept of navigating the maze.
- **Guest/Episode**: Dalton Caldwell, Michael Seibel (YC episodes)
- **Quotable lines**: "Most startup ideas are tarpit ideas" / "You're not lost, you're in the maze" / "The maze is the point"
- **Why meme-worthy**: Every founder in a pivot spiral relates to this. "Tarpit idea" is now common vocabulary.
- **Genre**: Dark indie folk, minor key — eerie and unsettling
- **Album**: Founders

## 4. "Ship It"
- **Iconic concept**: The bias toward shipping. Lenny's constant refrain about speed of iteration. Keith Rabois on "barrel vs ammunition" people and speed. Shishir Mehrotra on velocity.
- **Guest/Episode**: Keith Rabois, Shishir Mehrotra, Lenny's newsletter on shipping cadence
- **Quotable lines**: "If you're not embarrassed by the first version, you shipped too late" / "Speed is a proxy for how much you care" / "The best product managers ship, the rest plan"
- **Why meme-worthy**: The eternal tension between perfectionism and velocity. Tech Twitter's favorite debate.
- **Genre**: Lo-fi hip-hop, driving beat — urgency
- **Album**: Builders

## 5. "Taste"
- **Iconic concept**: Product sense / taste as the indefinable quality that separates good PMs from great ones. Shreyas Doshi's frameworks on product sense. The idea that taste can't be taught but can be developed.
- **Guest/Episode**: Shreyas Doshi, Brian Chesky (on obsessing over details), Jony Ive references
- **Quotable lines**: "Taste is the ability to say no to good ideas" / "Product sense is pattern recognition plus empathy" / "The details aren't the details — they make the product"
- **Why meme-worthy**: "Taste" has become the ultimate compliment in tech. "They have taste" = highest praise.
- **Genre**: Jazzy indie pop, sophisticated — Chet Baker vibes
- **Album**: Product

## 6. "Burn It Down (The Pivot Song)"
- **Iconic concept**: The moment you realize your current thing isn't working and you need to pivot. Slack was a gaming company. Instagram was Burbn. YouTube was a dating site.
- **Guest/Episode**: Stewart Butterfield (Slack pivot), various pivot stories across Lenny's archive
- **Quotable lines**: "The hardest decision isn't starting — it's stopping" / "Your ego is the last one to know" / "We killed our baby to build our company"
- **Why meme-worthy**: Every founder fears and romanticizes the pivot. It's tech's hero's journey.
- **Genre**: Indie rock, builds from quiet to loud — the breakthrough moment
- **Album**: Founders

## 7. "Do Less"
- **Iconic concept**: Prioritization as the #1 PM skill. Shreyas Doshi's LNO framework (Leverage, Neutral, Overhead). The counterintuitive idea that doing LESS creates more value.
- **Guest/Episode**: Shreyas Doshi (LNO framework), Lenny newsletters on prioritization, Jason Fried on saying no
- **Quotable lines**: "The best product managers are defined by what they say no to" / "Leverage, Neutral, Overhead — know which task is which" / "Strategy is choosing what not to do"
- **Why meme-worthy**: Every PM drowning in Jira tickets feels this. "LNO" is now standard PM vocabulary.
- **Genre**: Minimal synth-pop — sparse, intentional, every note matters
- **Album**: Product

## 8. "Talk to Users"
- **Iconic concept**: The most basic, most ignored advice in tech. Lenny's archive is FULL of stories where the answer was "did you actually talk to your users?" Teresa Torres on continuous discovery.
- **Guest/Episode**: Teresa Torres (continuous discovery), Gustaf Alstromer (YC on talking to users), multiple episodes
- **Quotable lines**: "The plural of anecdote is not data, but one conversation can save you six months" / "Go sit with your users" / "You're not your user"
- **Why meme-worthy**: It's become a meme BECAUSE everyone says it and no one does it.
- **Genre**: Acoustic pop, conversational — literally sounds like a conversation
- **Album**: Product

## 9. "The IC Track"
- **Iconic concept**: The Individual Contributor vs. Manager dilemma. Should you stay hands-on or go into management? Lenny's newsletter on career paths. The idea that IC is not "less than."
- **Guest/Episode**: Will Larson, Ravi Mehta (career frameworks), multiple Lenny newsletters
- **Quotable lines**: "Management is not a promotion" / "The best ICs create more value than most VPs" / "Choose the path that gives you energy, not status"
- **Why meme-worthy**: Every senior IC has had this crisis. Every manager has regretted the switch at 2am.
- **Genre**: Neo-soul, introspective — late night career reflection
- **Album**: The Human Side

## 10. "Imposter"
- **Iconic concept**: Imposter syndrome in tech. The universal experience of feeling like you're faking it. Particularly acute for PMs who "don't have a hard skill."
- **Guest/Episode**: Multiple guests have discussed this openly, Lenny's newsletter on imposter syndrome
- **Quotable lines**: "Everyone at the table thinks everyone else belongs more" / "The feeling never goes away, you just learn to work alongside it" / "Your imposter syndrome is lying to you"
- **Why meme-worthy**: The most relatable topic in all of tech. Everyone feels it, few admit it.
- **Genre**: Ambient folk, vulnerable — Bon Iver rawness
- **Album**: The Human Side

## 11. "First 100 Users"
- **Iconic concept**: How to get your first users. Lenny's most popular newsletter series ever. The tactical playbook: go where your users already are, do unscalable things, leverage your network.
- **Guest/Episode**: Lenny's "How the biggest companies got their first users" series, Gustaf Alstromer
- **Quotable lines**: "Your first 100 users should know your name" / "Steal from where attention already lives" / "If your first 100 users don't love you, your next 10,000 won't either"
- **Why meme-worthy**: This newsletter series was shared more than almost anything else Lenny wrote.
- **Genre**: Upbeat indie pop — optimistic, scrappy energy
- **Album**: Founders

- **Guest/Episode**: Casey Winters, Elena Verna, Brian Balfour

- **Genre**: Electronic, loop-based — literally about loops and retention
- **Album**: Builders

## 13. "The Onboarding Moment"

## 15. "Burnout Song"

## 16. "Metrics Don't Lie (But They Mislead)"
- **Iconic concept**: The dark side of data-driven decisions. When metrics become the goal instead of the signal. Goodhart's Law in practice.

- **Genre**: Electronic with jazz elements — structured chaos
- **Album**: Builders

## 17. "The Long Game"
- **Iconic concept**: Patience in building. The 10-year overnight success. Reid Hoffman's perspective on long-term thinking. Compounding.
- **Guest/Episode**: Reid Hoffman, Jeff Bezos references, Lenny on long-term career advice
- **Quotable lines**: "Most people overestimate what they can do in one year and underestimate what they can do in ten" / "The secret to overnight success is ten years of showing up"
