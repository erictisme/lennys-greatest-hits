## Killer Line
"Zuck was never doing this" — Brian Chesky's actual thought while hot-gluing cereal boxes for Airbnb. Funny, self-deprecating, universally relatable to anyone doing unglamorous work while comparing themselves to someone who made it. Works completely outside the song.

## Chord Progressions

**Verse: G - Em - C - D (I - vi - IV - V)**
Bright and forward-moving. The G major start keeps energy high for the rapid-fire storytelling. The Em adds a touch of vulnerability — these founders are out there getting rejected. The C-D resolution drives momentum, perfect for montage pacing.

**Chorus: C - G - Am - D (IV - I - ii - V)**
Opens on the IV chord for an anthemic lift — "Show up before you blow up" lands on a soaring, expansive sound. The Am on "every app you've ever loved" adds warmth and nostalgia. Resolves through D to set up the next verse or repeat.

**Bridge: Em - C - G - D (vi - IV - I - V)**
Starts reflective on the minor — the playbook is dying, things are shifting. "But somewhere in a dorm tonight" lifts back to G major, hope returning. The D ending builds tension before the final chorus release.
