## Killer Line
"They want a unicorn — I built a home"

## Lyrics

[Verse 1]
She closed my deck at slide eleven
Said, "Love the product, love the team"
"But the market caps at fifty million
And we write checks for bigger dreams"
Walked me to the lobby, shook my hand
Smiled like she was sorry for my plan

[Chorus]
Not venture-scale, not venture-scale
That's what they stamped across my name
But the users stayed, the revenue's real
They want a unicorn — I built a home

[Verse 2]
My buddy took the money, popped champagne
New floor, new title, everything looked great
But every board meeting moved the line
Now he builds for strangers, not for love
Owns ten percent of what was once his own
Golden treadmill, but it's not a home

[Chorus]
Not venture-scale, not venture-scale
That's what they stamped across his name
The freedom's gone, the pressure's always real
He got his unicorn — and lost his home

[Bridge]
Patrick kept it all — two hundred mil
Never raised a round, he owned the hill
Sold the whole thing, sat back, felt the chill
Whispered, "Left a billion on the table"
Even winners wonder what they missed

[Final Chorus]
Not venture-scale, not venture-scale
I know exactly what I am
The users stayed, and every cent is mine
They want a unicorn — I built a home

[Outro]
Not every fire needs to be the sun
Some fires just keep you warm

## Chord Progressions

**Verse: G - Cadd9 - Em7 - D**
Classic open folk progression. G to Cadd9 gives warmth, Em7 adds a thread of melancholy on the rejection lines, D resolves with quiet hope. Fingerpicked with open strings ringing — intimate and conversational, like telling a story over a beer.

**Chorus: C - G/B - Am7 - D**
Starting on C (IV) gives the chorus lift compared to the G-rooted verse. G/B creates a walking bass line descending (C - B - A), giving forward motion and momentum. Am7 adds the bittersweet quality — this isn't pure triumph, it's choosing your own path. Strummed, not picked — the song opens up here.

**Bridge: Em - C - G - D/F#**
Starts on Em (vi) for the emotional shadow of Patrick Campbell's twist — the poster child of bootstrapping who still wondered "what if." The bass walks chromatically (E - C - G - F#), creating unresolved tension. Doesn't fully resolve — hangs on D/F# before the final chorus lands back on G with certainty and peace.

## Self-Evaluation

### Lyric Craft Checklist
- [x] Show don't define — no thesis statements. The VC rejection is shown through scene (deck closing at slide eleven, lobby handshake, sorry smile). The treadmill is shown through buddy's story, not explained.
- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — all lines in 6-10 range, consistent within sections
- [x] One killer line identified — "They want a unicorn — I built a home"
- [x] Chorus varies on repetition — three versions: my name/his name/what I am; users stayed/freedom's gone/every cent is mine; I built a home/lost his home/I built a home (resolution)
- [x] No stat-stuffing — three numbers (fifty million, ten percent, two hundred mil) spread across the song, each serving narrative not data

### Suno Technical Checklist
- [x] Under 3000 characters total (~1200 characters of lyrics)
- [x] Section tags in [brackets]
- [x] No more than 1 verse before first chorus
- [x] Outro is short (2 lines)
- [x] Style prompt is detailed and specific

### Batch 1 Mistakes Avoided
- [x] Not preachy — the bridge introduces genuine doubt (Patrick's regret) instead of preaching bootstrapping good / VC bad
- [x] Real narrative — three characters (me rejected, buddy who took the money, Patrick who didn't) create a story arc, not a list of advice
- [x] Emotional — "smiled like she was sorry for my plan," "golden treadmill," "even winners wonder what they missed" carry feeling
- [x] Works as a song without source material — the unicorn/home contrast, golden treadmill, and fire/sun metaphor all land universally
