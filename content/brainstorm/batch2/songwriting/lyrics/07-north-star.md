## Chord Progression Notes

**Verse: Bm – G – D – A (vi – IV – I – V)**
The "searching" progression. Starting on the minor vi gives a restless, unsettled quality — mirrors drowning in dashboards. Resolves through D–A to keep pulling forward.

**Chorus: D – A – Bm – G (I – V – vi – IV)**
Classic anthemic pop progression. Starting on the tonic makes "Find your north star" land with declarative weight. The Bm–G at the end keeps a touch of yearning so it doesn't resolve too early.

**Bridge: Em – G – D/F# – A (ii – IV – I/3 – V)**
Darker and more tense. The ascending bass line (E → G → F# → A) builds urgency under the cautionary Facebook tale. Em adds a shadow neither verse nor chorus carries.

## Killer Line
"What you measure is what you become" — universally quotable, works completely outside the song. Captures the entire thesis in seven words without being a thesis statement.

## Runner-Up
"The metric worked — that's the thing you grieve" — ironic, chilling. Captures the Facebook cautionary tale in one devastating line. The juxtaposition of "worked" and "grieve" is the whole point.
