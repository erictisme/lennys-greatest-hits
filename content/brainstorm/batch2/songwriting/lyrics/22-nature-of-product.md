## Chord Progressions

**Verse: Em - C - G - D**
Classic rock progression in Em. The Em gives darkness and urgency matching the frustration. The C-G-D walkdown creates relentless forward motion — it feels like a machine you can't stop, which mirrors the feature factory grind.

**Chorus: Em - C - G - B7**
Same opening but the B7 replaces D, adding a dissonant, unresolved tension. B7 is the dominant of Em — it *wants* to resolve but feels unstable. Perfect for "the disease" — something that pulls at you, that won't let go.

**Bridge: Am - C - D - Em**
Starts on Am (the subdominant), which creates a shift — like opening a window. The C-D-Em build feels like climbing toward hope. This is the "give me one quarter" moment — asking for a chance. The resolution to Em grounds it: hope, but earned.

## Killer Line
"Who cares how fast you shipped what no one needs?" — quotable outside the song, captures the entire thesis in one punch. Works for anyone who's ever built something they didn't believe in.

## Key Refinements (from previous draft)
1. **Final chorus line 2**: "Push the answer down to those who know" → "The ones who feel it — let them find the way" — shows empowerment through imagery instead of stating the Netflix principle like a blog post
2. **Final chorus line 4**: "watch the builders grow" → "watch the builders stay" — mirrors "the builders leave" from Chorus 1, completing the arc (disease = they leave, cure = they stay)
3. **Bridge line 3**: "the people" → "the ones" — tighter, matches the rhythm of "one team, one problem, one real shot"
