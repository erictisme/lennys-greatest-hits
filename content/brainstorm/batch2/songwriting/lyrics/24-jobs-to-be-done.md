## Chord Progressions

**Verse: G - Em - C - D**
Open, storytelling progression. G-Em creates warmth, C-D provides gentle forward motion. The same progression Hozier uses for patient storytelling — no rush, just story.

**Chorus: C - G - Am - D**
Lifts from the verse by starting on C instead of G — a feeling of arrival and revelation. Am adds emotional weight on "You gave them what they asked for," and D holds tension on the unresolved "What's the job?"

**Bridge: Am - Em - C - G**
Starts minor for Bob's personal story — the vulnerability of not being able to read. Resolves back to G major, mirroring how his limitation became his strength. The Am-Em opening feels introspective before opening up.
