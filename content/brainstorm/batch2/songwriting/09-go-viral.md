## Chord Progressions

**Verse: F#m - D - A - E** (vi - IV - I - V in A major)
Drives forward through darkness — the minor root creates urgency while D - A - E rises toward hope. Matches the arc from 14 failures to the discovery moment.

**Chorus: D - A - E - F#m** (IV - I - V - vi)
Same chords starting on the bright D major — opens up into the spreading/triumph feeling. Resolving back to F#m keeps the edge, like triumph with an undercurrent of danger.

**Bridge: Bm - F#m - D - E** (ii - vi - IV - V)
Added tension for the "five years later, couldn't stop" admission. Bm pulls darker before the D - E lifts toward the final chorus release.

**Key: F#m** — Darker and more dramatic than the standard Em, works well with synth textures.
**BPM: 130** — Dance-adjacent energy, matches Nikita's manic intensity and the pace of viral spread.

## Killer Line

"Couldn't read a line, but I read the room"

Double meaning of "read" — couldn't read the Arabic text, but read the market signal perfectly. Works as standalone wisdom about intuition vs. information. Quotable outside any context.

- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — range is 7-12. One dialogue line at 11 (acceptable for quoted speech).
- [x] One killer line: "Couldn't read a line, but I read the room"
- [x] Chorus varies on repetition — three different second lines ("and then the next" / "rent and number one" / "lit up — then the rest"), two different closers ("watched it run" / "can't be undone")
- [x] No stat-stuffing — "fourteen," "forty percent," "eighteen hundred," "eighty" spread across the whole song, each serving narrative not data
