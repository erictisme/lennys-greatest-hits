## Story Arc

Karri Saarinen spent years watching the same scene play out at Airbnb, Coinbase, and every startup he touched: brilliant engineers forced to use clunky, soul-crushing project tools that made their work harder, not easier. In April 2019, he and two co-founders decided to build the tool they wished existed — Linear. But they didn't just build a better Jira. They built it a completely different *way*.

No product managers. No A/B tests. No metrics-based goals. No durable teams. Just taste, craft, and conviction. Engineers and designers think *and* execute — no middlemen translating vision into tickets. They launched in June 2020 with a few hundred companies already hooked from private beta, and nearly every single one converted to paid. They never doubled headcount in a single year. They spent $35K total on marketing. Ever.

By 2023, Linear had more cash in the bank than they'd ever raised in VC funding, had been profitable for 2+ years, and only two people had ever left the company. Block, Vercel, Ramp, Retool, Mercury — the fastest-growing companies in tech were all building with Linear. Karri proved that in a world obsessed with speed and scale, sometimes the answer is fewer people, stronger opinions, and an almost religious devotion to craft.

## Emotional Core

This is a rebellion song. It's about a designer who looked at the entire software industry — the dashboards full of metrics nobody reads, the A/B tests that optimize for nothing, the product managers who translate ideas into tickets instead of letting builders build — and said *no*. Not louder. Not faster. Not more. Just *better*. The tension is between the modern tech machine (move fast, measure everything, hire armies, scale at all costs) and the craftsman's creed (fewer people, stronger opinions, taste over data, slow down to go fast). It's the feeling of caring about something everyone else has given up on — the invisible detail, the context menu that doesn't flicker, the animation that feels *right*. In a world that rewards shipping slop, Linear chose to make something beautiful.

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches

Trust your taste over your metrics — the best products come from people who care deeply enough to say "this doesn't feel right yet" instead of "the numbers say ship it."
