## The ONE Emotional Truth
Every story is a five-second moment of change. The days you don't name are the days you lose.

## Killer Line
> "Time doesn't fly — it goes unaccounted"

## Critique Log (Rick Rubin Mode — before final rewrite)

**Problems found in Draft 1:**
1. "1.8 moments — year one" — Suno will mangle this; number too fragmented
2. "I know what living's for" — thesis-statement, not imagery; cut
3. "Everything before was preparation / For a thing you earn" — explaining the lesson; cut
4. "Write it down before it vanishes" = 9 syllables with a mouthful consonant cluster on "vanishes"
5. Eileen's bridge introduced abruptly — needed grounding in specific place/action
6. Pre-chorus "Is a day erased" — too weak, vague
7. "The longest mile" as filler — replaced with a harder image
8. Stat-stuffing: two numbers in one verse

**Fixes applied:**
- Removed "1.8" stat; kept concept without number
- "I know what living's for" → replaced with "The days don't feel the same" (shows, doesn't define)
- "Everything before was preparation / For a thing you earn" → replaced with "She'd been carrying it for years / And now she let it shine"
- Chorus line 3 simplified to "Write it down — or lose the day" (7 syl, clean for falsetto)
- Bridge grounded with classroom setting + Eileen's direct quote
- Pre-chorus close: "Never comes back out" — short drop line for impact
- Only ONE number in the song: "Twelve years in"
- Killer line promoted to anchor position in Verse 2: "Time doesn't fly — it goes unaccounted"
