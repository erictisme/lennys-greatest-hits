## Rick Rubin Self-Critique

### What works:
- Castle/moat metaphor is vivid, physical, singable — carries the whole song
- "Treadmill" in V1 vs. "castle" in chorus = clear emotional contrast
- 6/8 time signature gives the anthemic, sweeping quality the framework deserves
- V2 shows counter positioning and network effects WITHOUT naming them ("What I carried they couldn't carry" = counter positioning; "the people started staying" = network/switching)
- Bridge distills 7 powers to "seven walls" — evocative without being a textbook
- Final chorus variation: "something real" replaces "something" — earned emotional payoff
- End-stopped lines throughout — every line stands alone
- Syllable range: 6-9, consistent within sections

### What I fixed from first instincts:
- Removed all framework jargon (no "counter positioning," "scale economies," "switching costs")
- No Netflix example — too corporate for folk anthem
- No stat-stuffing — zero numbers except "seven" in the bridge (the title demands it)
- Didn't quote Buffett directly — absorbed the castle/moat image into original lyrics
- V2 SHOWS the powers through scenes: giants stumbling (counter positioning), people staying (network/switching), walls growing with time (scale)
- Killer line: "You're not slow — you're building something real" — the reassurance every grinding founder needs

### Remaining risk:
- "Changed the climb" in V2 is slightly abstract — but it works as a metaphor for redefining the competitive landscape
- Bridge is 5 lines instead of 4 — the extra line ("Something worth defending... more") is intentional for the build, but watch Suno interpretation
