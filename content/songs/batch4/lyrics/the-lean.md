## One Emotional Truth
A movement that changed the world, looking back with pride and regret — the man who taught others to fail fast couldn't control where his words traveled.

## Killer Line
> "I thought it was obvious / I thought we all would care"

## Notes for Producer

- The 3/4 waltz feel should be slow and unhurried — a congregation processing, not a march
- Verse 1 should feel almost confessional, intimate — just guitar, maybe a single piano note
- The pre-chorus is where the acoustic guitar blooms, piano starts to swell
- Chorus: community vocals enter, warm and unison — not harmonized yet
- Bridge: strip back to solo voice, almost a cappella, just one guitar note per beat
- "Ohhhh, I thought we all would care" — the gang vocal swells here like a hymn response
- Final chorus: full band, full congregation, but still gentle — 72 BPM never speeds up
- Outro: piano alone, the guitar has left, ending on an unresolved question
