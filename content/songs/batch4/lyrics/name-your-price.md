## Self-Critique (Rick Rubin Mode)

### Sing Test: PASS
- Lines are conversational, open vowels on key words ("price," "right," "night," "made," "rate," "away")
- Chorus is immediately singable — 4 lines, natural melody arc
- "Name your price" hook is rhythmic and memorable

### Blog Test: PASS
- No thesis statements ("pricing is..." / "pricing means...")
- Shows the behavior (looked away, dropped the price, walked away) rather than defining concepts
- "Three levers on the wall" is imagery, not a lecture

### Universal Test: PASS
- Non-tech reading: this is about undervaluing your work, being afraid to ask for what you're worth
- "Polished every pixel" works literally for tech, metaphorically for any craft
- "Exchange rate on the world you made" is universally resonant

### Filler Test: PASS
- Every line earns its place — no "oh yeah" or "that's the key" filler
- Bridge is lean (4 lines) and delivers the Patrick Campbell quote as imagery

### Syllable Test: PASS
- All lines 6-10 syllables
- Consistent within sections (Verse 1: 9/8/9/9/9/8/8/8, Verse 2: 9/10/9/10/10/9/9/8)
- Chorus: 6/9/9/10 — tight and singable

### Chorus Test: PASS
- Chorus 1 and 2 identical
- Final chorus changes only one word: "so" --> "now" (emotional payoff — from explaining to demanding)

### End-Stopped Test: PASS
- Every line is a complete thought
- No run-on lines or dangling phrases

### Stat-Stuffing Test: PASS
- Only one number-adjacent reference: "three levers" in V1
- "Every quarter" in V2 is the only other metric reference
- Facts are woven into imagery, not listed

### Jargon Test: PASS
- "Exchange rate" is universal language
- "Revenue per customer" in V2 is the one semi-technical line — but it reads as plain English
- No insider acronyms or framework names

## Production Notes

- **Sonic Signature:** Muted jazz guitar (Tom Misch-style) with Rhodes keys creating a late-night groove. The guitar should have that warm, slightly overdriven clean tone — not crisp, not crunchy.
- **Drums:** Brushed kit with a neo-soul pocket, tasteful hi-hat work. NOT a standard rock beat.
- **Bass:** Upright bass throughout, prominent in the bridge where everything else drops out.
- **Vocal:** Warm male tenor, intimate close-mic, conversational delivery. Slight rasp on emotional peaks.
- **Build:** Verse 1 is sparse (Rhodes + guitar). Verse 2 adds brushed drums + bass. Bridge strips to upright bass + voice only. Final chorus brings everything back with subtle string pad.
- **Time feel:** Straight but with a laid-back, behind-the-beat swing feel. Not metronomic.
