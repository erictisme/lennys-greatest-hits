TITLE: Seven Powers
STYLE: Anthemic indie folk in 6/8 time, D major, 88 BPM. Warm male baritone vocal, intimate and earnest in verses, building to full-throated conviction in choruses. Fingerpicked nylon acoustic guitar opens alone, stomping floor kick enters at chorus in 6/8 pulse. Second verse adds second acoustic guitar (steel string strumming). Bridge builds with swelling violin and sustained cello drone. Final chorus explodes with gang vocals, full strumming, tambourine, and layered harmonies. Outro decays to solo fingerpicking. Warm analog production, close-mic vocals, room reverb. Think Hollow Coves "Coastline" meets CityAlight hymn energy. NOT aggressive — triumphant but tender. Violin is the star instrument from bridge onward.
SOURCE: Business strategy with Hamilton Helmer (author of 7 Powers) (Hamilton Helmer, podcast)
---
LYRICS:

[Verse 1, fingerpicked guitar, intimate]
I was running every morning,
Chasing numbers down the hall,
Shipping fast and hiring faster,
Swore I'd never hit a wall.
But the treadmill kept on spinning,
And the ground beneath me stayed —
Everything I built by sundown,
Someone else had built by May.

[Chorus, stomping 6/8 kick, full strum]
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
It's not the speed that keeps you standing,
It's what they can never take back.
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
You're not slow — you're building something,
Something no one else can match.

[Verse 2, add steel string guitar]
I watched the giants stumble sideways,
Couldn't follow where I'd gone.
What I carried they couldn't carry —
Cost too much to change their song.
Then the people started staying,
Every wall grew tall with time.
Not because I ran the fastest,
But because I changed the climb.

[Chorus, stomping 6/8 kick, full strum]
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
It's not the speed that keeps you standing,
It's what they can never take back.
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
You're not slow — you're building something,
Something no one else can match.

[Bridge, building, violin enters]
Seven walls around this ground,
Every one a different door.
I don't need to run forever —
Just need something worth defending,
Something worth defending... more.

[Final Chorus, full band, gang vocals]
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
It's not the speed that keeps you standing,
It's what they can never take back.
Lay the stone and dig the moat,
Build a castle, not a shack.
You're not slow — you're building something real,
Something no one else can match.

[Outro, decaying to solo fingerpicking]
Lay the stone... dig the moat...
