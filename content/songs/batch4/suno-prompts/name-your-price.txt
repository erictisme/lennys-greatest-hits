TITLE: Name Your Price

STYLE: Jazz-pop neo-soul, Bb major, 92 BPM. Warm male tenor vocal, close-mic intimate delivery, conversational tone with slight rasp on peaks. Muted clean jazz guitar (Tom Misch style), Rhodes electric piano, upright bass, brushed drum kit with neo-soul pocket. Laid-back behind-the-beat swing feel. Verse 1 sparse — just Rhodes and fingerpicked guitar. Verse 2 adds brushed drums and upright bass. Bridge strips down to upright bass and voice only. Final chorus brings full arrangement back with subtle warm string pad underneath. Jazz chord voicings — maj7, m9, add13. Late-night sophisticated groove, not aggressive. Think Tom Misch meets Chet Baker with Jacob Collier harmonic color. Warm analog production, vinyl warmth, room ambience.

SOURCE: "10 lessons on bootstrapping a $200m business" (Patrick Campbell, podcast) + "How to price your product" (Naomi Ionita, podcast) + "Pricing your SaaS product" (Patrick Campbell, newsletter)
