## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Pete Kazanjy comes from product marketing and product management at VMware — zero sales background. He starts a recruiting software company (TalentBin) in 2009 and realizes "this B2B software stuff doesn't sell itself." He has to become the first seller, sales manager, and sales leader all at once.

**Middle / Turning Point:** The realization — "Man, this isn't rocket surgery, anyone can learn this. If I can learn this, other people can learn this." He reframes sales from sleazy Glengarry Glen Ross stereotypes into something modern: you're not selling ice to an Eskimo, you're a consultant helping people solve real problems. The discomfort of meeting new humans every single day, forming rapport in 90 seconds, asking strangers for money — it's learnable, like updating source code.

**End:** Pete writes the book "Founding Sales" — the book he wished he had. Builds Modern Sales Pros (30,000-member community). The insight scales: founder does it first, packages it, hands it to 2 reps, then 4, then 8. The "while loop" of sales. The score takes care of itself.

## Emotional Core

**The human tension:** A technical person — someone who lives in code, in systems, in logic — must step into the most human, most uncomfortable, most rejection-filled activity in business: selling. The identity shift from builder to seller. The vulnerability of cold-calling strangers. The cringe of asking for money. And discovering that it's not magic, it's a learnable skill — like debugging, but with humans.

**Feeling:** Liberation through discomfort. The relief of realizing you don't have to be a "born seller." The humor of a coder treating sales conversations like software loops.

---

## The ONE Thing

You don't have to be great at sales — you just have to stop being afraid of it, because nobody is born knowing how to sell.
