## 1. Story Arc

**Beginning:** Jeanne joins Google right when Gmail launches in 2004. Software felt invincible — Gmail had a whole year of technical lead over Yahoo Mail. She starts to wonder: what happens when software stops being the differentiator?

**Turning Point:** A decade later, that realization hits. Cloud computing commoditizes tech. Products converge at the margin. The experience of being sold to becomes the new moat. She carries this thesis into Stripe, then Vercel.

**Middle (Stripe Era):** At Stripe, she builds the early sales team from scratch. Her operating constraint: "efficiency is leverage" — the company gives her 4 SDRs where others would use 30. She tries to build "Project Rosland" in 2017 — a massive company universe database to make outbound hyper-targeted — but the AI doesn't exist yet to make it work. She does a whiteboarding session instead of a boring discovery call. Sales that doesn't feel like sales.

**Now (Vercel Era):** Eight years later she's at Vercel doing the same thing — and this time, AI makes it work. A single GTM engineer rebuilds Project Rosland in 6 weeks, spending 25-30% of his time. 10 SDRs become 1. The lead agent costs $1,000/year to run versus $1M+ in salaries. She's invented the "deal-bott" — an AI that reads Gong call transcripts and finds what humans miss.

**End:** The promise of GTM as a product: from the first awareness touch to a 5-year, wall-to-wall customer — every step intentional, human, and unique. Not transactions. Experiences.

## 2. Specific Numbers & Facts

- **10 SDRs → 1**: After the lead agent went live, 9 SDRs were redeployed to outbound
- **$1,000/year**: Cost to run the lead agent for the entire year (vs. $1M+ in salaries)
- **6 weeks**: Time it took one GTM engineer (25-30% of his time) to build the lead agent
- **2017**: Year Jeanne first tried to build "Project Rosland" at Stripe — too early for AI
- **30% to 40%**: Percent of time salespeople actually spend in front of customers (consistent for 20 years)
- **70%**: Target — AI handles rote work so salespeople can spend 70% with humans
- **80%**: Of customers buy to avoid pain or reduce risk, not to increase upside
- **$1M ARR**: Rule of thumb for when to hire your first salesperson
- **~10 people**: Good scale to start thinking about GTM engineering
- **78%**: Forbes AI 50 companies that use Stripe
- **50%+**: Fortune 100 that use Stripe
- **1 year**: Technical lead Gmail had over Yahoo Mail after launch in 2004
- **Lyft, Instacart, DoorDash**: All built on Stripe for marketplace payments
- **OpenAI**: Top 25 traffic site on the internet — at Vercel, that bumps them from mid-market to enterprise
- **17**: Number of different roles that exist within go-to-market today (being redefined)
- **August**: When Vercel did a major pricing restructure, separating enterprise from startup tiers

## 4. Emotional Core

**The human tension:** You're trying to close a deal, but what the customer needs is a partner, not a closer. The best salespeople disappear — they look like product managers, consultants, builders. The irony is: the less you feel like sales, the more you sell.

**Underlying feeling:** The loneliness of the early sales grind — 4 SDRs where everyone else has 30, spreadsheets instead of AI, trying to map the whole universe of companies by hand in 2017 and failing. And then the joy of watching that same vision work 8 years later, in weeks, for $1,000.

**Secondary tension:** AI is coming for SDR jobs — but Jeanne frames it as liberation. You didn't go to college to do rote prospecting. Now you don't have to. The human capacity gets unleashed, not replaced.

---

## 5. The ONE Thing

**GTM is not just sales — it's the full customer experience, from first awareness to five-year loyalty, and the best teams design it like a product and build it with AI.**

## 9. Songwriting Notes

- **Narrative angle to use:** Jeanne's POV as a builder — the 2017 failure (Project Rosland) and the 2025 success is the emotional spine. "I tried this once before, it wasn't time / now the machine learns and the data rhymes."
- **Key imagery:** Whiteboarding sessions instead of interrogations. Maps of companies on a screen. A single engineer with 25% of his time and 6 weeks. Welts from a diving board, getting back up.
- **Avoid:** Generic "hustle" sales energy. This song should feel intelligent, not aggressive. The persona is a builder, not a bro.
- **Hook potential phrase:** "Make a dollar, touch a soul" — plays on her definition of GTM but flips the emotional register
- **Structural suggestion:** Verse tells the Stripe story (constrained, scrappy), pre-chorus pivots to the AI unlock, chorus lands the GTM philosophy. Bridge could be the diving board / resilience moment.
