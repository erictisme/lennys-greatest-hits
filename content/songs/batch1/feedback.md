# Batch 1 Feedback

11 songs generated. Full audit in `content/brainstorm/batch1-audit.md`.

## What Went Well
- Strong hooks — "You'll Know," "Ship it," "Vibe coding," "Talk to your users" are all memorable
- Emotional range — joy (PMF), fear (Imposter), exhaustion (Burnout), wonder (Eleven Stars)
- Story specificity — when songs use real stories (Airbnb couches, Rahul's 22→58 journey), they work
- Best line: "You're reading sheet music but you've never heard the song" (Talk to Users) — metaphor does all the work

## What Went Wrong
- **Thesis-statement choruses** in 8/11 songs — lines like "Burnout isn't working too much / It's working on the wrong things" read like blog posts
- **Zero chorus variation** across all 11 songs — every chorus repeats word-for-word
- **Stat-stuffing** in 7 songs — cramming numbers into 12+ syllable lines causes Suno mumbling
- **Insider references** — "Feather, brick, dump truck," "Paloaltodelivery dot com," "Lenny's Slack" alienate outside listeners
- **Filler rhymes** — "no fears," "that's the key," "go take a look" exist only to complete a rhyme
- **Metronomic couplets** — many verses feel mechanical with identical line lengths

## Rules for Next Batch
1. No line should read like a blog post definition. If it defines a concept, rewrite it as an image or scene.
2. Every chorus must vary on repetition — change at least one line, better to change the emotional angle.
3. Maximum 12 syllables per line. Count them. If over 12, split or cut.
4. No insider jargon unless it's the song's central hook (like "vibe coding").
5. Every line must earn its place. If it exists only to rhyme, cut it and use a slant rhyme or break the pattern.
6. One killer line per song that works outside the context of Lenny's Newsletter.
7. Add a critique step before finalizing — read every line aloud and flag anything that sounds like a LinkedIn caption.
