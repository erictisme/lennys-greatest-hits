## Chord Progressions

### Verse: Am - F - C - G (i - VI - III - VII)
The natural minor i - VI establishes gravity immediately — melancholic but not hopeless. The C - G lift creates forward pull so the verse doesn't stall. This is the "watching the dashboard at 3am" sound: heavy but moving.

### Pre-Chorus: F - C - G - Am (VI - III - VII - i)
Resolves back to Am, which signals "we're not out of the dark yet." The pre-chorus raises tension — the founder has tried everything — before the chorus delivers the gut-punch.

### Chorus: Am - F - C - G, then C - G - Am (landing hard on Am)
Each "They don't come back" lands on Am. The resolution to the minor tonic feels like the truth arriving. It's not triumphant — it's reckoning.

### Bridge: Am - G - F - E (i - VII - VI - V)
The E (major V in minor key) creates a moment of unexpected brightness — "that flat line, that's the thing that matters most of all." The harmonic surprise mirrors the emotional reframe: the flat line isn't failure, it's the signal you've been waiting for.

**Why Am at 88 BPM:** Am carries natural gravity — it's the key of honest reckoning without despair. 88 BPM is walking tempo — deliberate, forward-moving, heavy enough to feel like late-night thinking. The fingerpicked guitar in Am naturally produces those open-string resonances that make acoustic songs feel intimate and lived-in.

## Killer Line

> There's a point where the line goes flat — that's the moment you've been building at

Paraphrases Dan Hockenmaier's insight about cohort retention asymptoting and Casey Winters' "great retention is THE scalable way to grow." Transforms a technical metric into a universal truth: the flatness is the destination. You're not looking for the spike — you're looking for the floor.

## Quality Checklist

### Lyric Craft
- [x] Show don't define — the 3am dashboard is shown, not explained. "Two-thirds of January" is a scene, not a stat-dump.
- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — all lines 7-12 syllables. Bridge intentionally runs slightly longer for emotional weight.
- [x] One killer line — bridge lines 1-2, surrounded by quieter lines
- [x] Chorus varies on each repetition — all three choruses change lines 2, 4, and 5
- [x] Max 2 numbers per verse — Verse 1: two-thirds, Verse 2: eight percent + forty-five. Bridge and chorus: zero numbers
- [x] No filler rhymes — "growing pains / explains" was cut after critique and replaced; every rhyme earns its place
- [x] End-stopped lines — read each line aloud, each is a complete phrase
- [x] Concrete imagery — dashboard, three in the morning, the line, the flat curve
- [x] Slant rhymes over perfect — "clean / fourteen", "brand / land", "call / fall", "twice / nice / line"

### Suno Technical
- [x] Under 3000 characters total
- [x] Section tags in [brackets] with one performance cue per section
- [x] One verse before first chorus
- [x] Outro is short (4 lines)
- [x] Style prompt detailed with artist refs + instruments + vocal character + BPM + key + mood

### Batch Mistakes Avoided
- [x] Not preachy — no "retention is important" thesis lines
- [x] Real narrative arc — 3am denial → tried everything → reckoning → reframe (flat line = destination)
- [x] Emotional — late-night anxiety, the patient saint metaphor, the bridge revelation
- [x] Works as standalone song — "they don't come back" is universal grief, not just product grief
- [x] No insider jargon — "cohort," "CURR," "asymptote" never appear
- [x] No stat-stuffing — two numbers in verse 2, spread across four lines
