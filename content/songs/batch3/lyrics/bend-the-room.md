**Sources:** "A PM's guide to influence" (Jules Walter), "How to get better at influence" (Lenny Rachitsky)
**Emotional core:** The quiet confidence of someone who influences through listening, not force
**Killer line:** "I learned to speak in your language first"

## Quality Critique
- **Sing Test:** Flows naturally, open vowels on key words (room, fold, bend, need). Pre-chorus is catchy and memorable.
- **Blog Test:** No thesis statements. V2 tells Slack story through scene, not definition. Bridge uses Carnegie quote as imagery.
- **Universal Test:** Works as a song about persuasion in any context — could be about negotiation, relationships, leadership.
- **Filler Test:** Every line advances the story or emotional arc. No padding.
- **Chorus Test:** Three variations — "your side/plays," "fold/tossed," "need/bend" — each escalates confidence.
