# Ask for Help

**Album:** The Human Side
**Source:** Natalie Rothfels — "On asking for help (even when you really don't want to)" (Lenny's Newsletter, Aug 2024)
**Genre:** CityAlight hymn-like simplicity meets Hollow Coves warmth
**BPM:** ~82 | **Key:** G major
**Killer Line:** "I worked ten times harder than the ask would ever cost"

---

[Verse 1, gentle acoustic]
Sat before the blank screen, five PM
Everyone went home, I stayed again
Chest got tight, crept up to my throat
Couldn't write a single honest note

[Verse 2]
First one in and last one standing tall
Answered every message, took each call
Built my name on never needing rest
Wore the weight and called it being blessed

[Chorus 1]

[Verse 3]
Worked ten times harder than the ask

The fear said weakness looks like death
But silence stole my every breath

[Chorus]
I don't have to carry this alone

[Bridge, building warmth]
They didn't see me as a burden

[Chorus, congregational, full band]
We don't have to carry this alone

Open hands, three words, begin —
Will you help us? Let us in

[Outro, stripped back]
Three words... that changed everything
Three words... I'm still learning

## Critique & Revision Notes

**Sing Test:** Read aloud — hymn structure carries it. "Would you help me? Let me in" is singable on first listen, easy to join in. The chorus shifts from "I" to "we" by the end, creating congregational momentum. Pass.

**Blog Test:** No thesis statements. "Asking for help is strength" is never stated — it's shown through Meena's story (blank screen, chest tight, the response of her peers leaning in). The lesson lives in the bridge scene, not a definition. Pass.

**Universal Test:** Anyone who's stayed late pretending they're fine, who's carried too much because asking felt worse than drowning — this lands without knowing Lenny or Natalie. Pass.

**Filler Test:** Every line carries story or image. "Let me in" does double duty — asking to be let into help, and asking to let vulnerability in. Pass.

**Syllable Test:** All lines 6-10 syllables. Pass.

**Chorus Test:** Three variations — "carry/prove/carry" with different second lines. Pronoun shift from "I" to "we" in final chorus. "Let me in / I'll begin / Come on in" — three different emotional angles on the same ask. Pass.
