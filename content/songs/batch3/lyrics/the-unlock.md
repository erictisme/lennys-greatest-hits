## Chord Progressions

### Verse: C - Am - F - G (I - vi - IV - V)
Classic dream-pop movement — the C opens warm and open, Am adds introspection, F is the widening horizon, G pushes forward. The cyclical feel mirrors the process of narrowing down signals: you keep returning to the same question from a slightly different angle.

### Chorus: F - G - Am - C (IV - V - vi - I)
Starts on the IV (F) for that immediate lift — the "find the unlock" line floats up on the F chord before resolving home. Am gives the hook a slightly wistful quality (you haven't found it yet — this is the search). Resolves to C with certainty.

### Bridge: Am - F - C - G (vi - IV - I - V)
Opens on the minor vi — the quiet accusation that most teams never look. Then F-C opens up with warmth as the habit insight emerges. G at the end is a suspended push into the final chorus, where the search becomes a find.

**Why C major at 96 BPM:** C major is the clearest, most open key — it has no sharps or flats and naturally sounds unencumbered. 96 BPM is lo-fi territory: slow enough to feel thoughtful, fast enough to feel alive. Matches the curious-but-unhurried energy of a builder finally narrowing toward the answer.

## Killer Line
> But somewhere in the first few days / A habit forms behind the haze

This is the moment the detective story resolves. "Behind the haze" is both literal (it's hidden in data noise) and emotional (the fog of early product work). It reframes activation from a metric into a human moment — the user forming a quiet commitment they don't even know they're making yet.
