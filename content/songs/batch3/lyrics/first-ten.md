## Killer Line
**"They weren't looking when you found them"**
The counter-intuitive truth at the center: the best people are never available. You have to earn them.

## Second Killer Line
**"You know you've got the pitch exactly right / When they offer to invest"**
(Siqi Chen quote, sung — the moment of proof)

## Chorus Evolution
- Chorus 1: Introduces the pattern — "You didn't post the role, you wrote the letter"
- Chorus 2: The moment of proof — "When they offer to invest"
- Chorus 3: The payoff — "You didn't hire a team — you built a culture"

## Critique Notes (Pre-Rewrite)

**What worked:**
- "The room fills up like that" — earned brevity, earthy image
- Bridge line "Don't lower the bar for anyone" — Nick Handel quote, lands as command not thesis
- Outro "The first ten rooms you build / Become the house" — visual, quotable, hymn-appropriate closing
- All three choruses carry distinct weight — not identical repetition

**What was tightened:**
- Pre-chorus line 2 was "Asking them to fall before the walls even go up" → cut to 12 syl, still fine
- "The third one knows a fourth who knows a fifth" — shows the snowball numerically, specific and visual
- Removed stat-stuffing ("100 hours" appears only in process notes, not lyrics)
- "You didn't hire a team — you built a culture" placed in final chorus only, not verse — earns its thesis position
