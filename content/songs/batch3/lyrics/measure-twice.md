# Measure Twice
Album: By the Numbers
Genre: Jazz-pop
Mood: Patient, cool, quietly devastating
BPM: 82
Key: Dm
Reference: Tom Misch (jazz-pop sophistication, groove), Chet Baker (cool intimacy, patience, late-night mood)
Source: "How to measure cohort retention" + "How to increase your product's retention" — Lenny Rachitsky
Topics: Retention, cohort analysis, product-market fit, onboarding, metrics, patience
JTBD Category: Making better product decisions through data
Chord Progression: Dm – Am – Bb – C | Gm – Dm – Am – C | Dm – Bb – Am – Gm

## Chord Notes
- **Verse: Dm – Am – Bb – C (i – v – VI – VII)** — Natural minor movement. Restless and searching. The unresolved VII (C natural) keeps things hanging — mirrors watching a curve that hasn't decided where it's going yet.
- **Chorus: Gm – Dm – Am – C (iv – i – v – VII)** — Starting on the subdominant (Gm) gives a sigh quality, like releasing held breath. Returns to Dm for weight. The C at the end pulls back to the top without ever fully resolving — appropriate for a song about waiting.
- **Bridge: Dm – Bb – Am – Gm (i – VI – v – iv)** — Descending. The stepwise bass motion (D → Bb → A → G) creates a slow revelation feel, like a confession. The pull downward mirrors the confession that the measurement was wrong all along.

## Killer Line
"A flat line means someone stayed" — counterintuitive (flat lines are usually bad news), jazz-deadpan delivery makes it land sideways. The whole insight in six words.

## Self-Evaluation

### Lyric Craft
- [x] Show don't define — "watching people walk out the door" not "retention is the rate at which users return"
- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — all main verse/chorus lines 7-10 syl; short outro lines are intentional punctuation
- [x] One killer line identified — "A flat line means someone stayed"
- [x] Chorus varies across repetitions — C1: general watching/leaving frame, C2: flattening moment + the "will to leave" tension, Final: resolution with the 10% affirmation
- [x] No stat-stuffing — "seventh day, thirtieth" is concrete without explaining methodology; no percentages crammed mid-line
- [x] End-stopped lines — each line is a complete breath/thought
- [x] Open vowels on key singing moments — "curve," "time," "stayed," "door," "rain," "air"
- [x] Slant rhymes over perfect — "week/seek," "same/rain," "most/most" (echo), "ads/had"
