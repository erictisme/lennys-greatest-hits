## Chord Progressions

**Key: G major | BPM: 104**

**Verse: G - D - Em - C (I - V - vi - IV)**
The workhorse of indie folk warmth — used here because the verse is observational and grounded. G gives it stability (the steady interviewer). The walk through D to Em creates gentle forward lean, like leaning across the table. C resolves back without drama. Fingerpicked, not strummed — intimate not anthemic.

**Chorus: C - G - D - G (IV - I - V - I)**
Starting on C (the IV) lifts the chorus above the verse — the question is an opening, not a confrontation. The move back to G via D feels like arrival. Simple and singable. On "Tell me what hard means to you" — land on the G with the word "you" every time. Strumming enters here, light percussion.

**Bridge: Em - D - C - G (vi - V - IV - I)**
The minor vi entry on Em creates a moment of vulnerability — this is where the song zooms out and asks the deeper questions. The walk back down to G via D and C mirrors the candidate letting the walls down, returning to something real. One lyrical build (no percussion drop) before the final chorus swells.
