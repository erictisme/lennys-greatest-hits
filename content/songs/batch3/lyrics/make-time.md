## Chord Progressions

### Verse: G - Em - C - D (I - vi - IV - V)
Classic folk-pop warmth. G opens with optimism, Em adds gentle emotion (the sticky note, the stirring), C grounds with sincerity, D pushes forward. The progression has that Stephen Day quality — simple enough to sing along, rich enough to carry weight.

### Chorus: C - D - Em - G (IV - V - vi - I)
Starts on C for immediate warmth — the question "what's the highlight" floats up naturally. D builds, Em gives the "one good moment" line its tender vulnerability. Resolves to G with quiet certainty. The vi-I movement at the end (Em to G) mirrors the shift from searching to settling.

### Bridge: Em - C - G - D (vi - IV - I - V)
Opens on Em for the "busy bandwagon" observation — slightly outside, looking in. C-G opens up as the insight lands. D at the end suspends into the final chorus, where the answer arrives.
