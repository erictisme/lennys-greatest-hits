# Ninety-Three Percent

## Song Info
- **Source:** How to pass any first-round interview (even in a terrible talent market) (Coach Erika, Newsletter)
- **Album:** Career Growth
- **Genre:** Lo-fi hip hop / spoken word hybrid
- **BPM:** 88
- **Key:** Bb minor
- **Killer Line:** "You're not learning lines — you're remembering who you are"

## Rick Rubin Critique

**What works:**
- The ironed shirt / cold coffee opening is concrete and immediate — you're IN the scene
- "Walk it back" as a hook is rhythmically strong and ties to the memory lane concept
- Call-and-response format suits the lo-fi hip hop genre
- Pre-chorus contrast (whispered vs. spoken) creates dynamic range
- Verse 2's mirroring concept is woven in naturally ("use their words like they're my own")

**What needs work:**
1. "That's the throne" in V2 is a filler rhyme — doesn't earn its place
2. "Every scar becomes a signal / When you frame it like you won" — too polished, too LinkedIn. Needs grit.
3. Bridge line "Every mark became a star" is cliché and saccharine
4. "Ninety-three percent don't crack" — what does "don't crack" mean here? Unclear.
5. V1 line "Through the stories I've been living" is vague — show which stories
6. Pre-chorus lines are good but the second one ("clean performance") is weaker than the first ("perfect stranger")

**The honest question:** Does this song FEEL like 3 a.m. interview prep? The opening does. But by the chorus it becomes a motivational poster. The best lo-fi hip hop stays in the room — keep the listener in that apartment, at that desk, with those nerves.

## Second Critique

**Improved:** Bridge now references STAR naturally, V1 "launch that almost broke me" is specific, "one good reason stands alone" is cleaner.

**Still wrong:** V2 last line "Back to ground where I have stands" is awkward/ungrammatical — forced rhyme. Fix it.

## Quality Checklist
- [x] Show don't define — no thesis statements
- [x] 5-8 syllables per line (lo-fi hip hop range, some 7-8 for flow)
- [x] Killer line: "You're not learning lines — you're remembering who you are"
- [x] Chorus varies on each repetition (3 variations)
- [x] Max 2 numbers per verse (only "five" in V1, "hundred" in V2)
- [x] No filler rhymes
- [x] Concrete imagery (ironed shirt, cold coffee, mirror practice)
- [x] End-stopped lines
- [x] Under 3000 chars
- [x] No insider jargon
- [x] Rhyme pattern: every 2nd or 4th line
- [x] Syllable counts consistent within sections
- [x] Chorus shorter/simpler than verse
- [x] One performance cue per section tag
