TITLE: First Ten
STYLE: liturgical folk, hymn, acoustic indie, CityAlight, Hollow Coves, warm congregational, intimate
MOOD: founding anthem, earnest, warm, slow build, communal
BPM: 92
KEY: D major
INSTRUMENTS: acoustic fingerpicked guitar, light organ pads, brushed snare, warm bass enters verse 2, full band swells at bridge
VOCALS: warm male tenor, close-mic, intimate delivery, breathy on verses, full open tone on choruses
PRODUCTION: lo-fi warmth, room ambience, analog feel, gentle reverb, not overproduced

---

[Intro — acoustic guitar, fingerpicked, solo]

[Verse 1]
Empty room and a half-built plan
Two years failing, still no traction
Four guys nobody wants to bet on
Making calls that no one's answering

[Pre-Chorus]
You're asking someone to risk it all
Asking them to fall before the walls are up

[Chorus]
They weren't looking when you found them
Slow and steady, six long months
You didn't post the role, you wrote the letter
One by one until you're ten

[Verse 2]
The first one builds the thing you can't
The second checks your work for cracks
The third one knows a fourth who knows a fifth
The room fills up like that

[Pre-Chorus]
A hundred hours before each yes
You earn the trust before you get the desk

[Chorus — Variation 2]
They weren't looking when you found them
Slow and steady, six long months
You know you've got the pitch exactly right
When they offer to invest

[Bridge — full band enters, organ swells]
Great people pull great people in
You can't fake what's real to them
Don't lower the bar for anyone
The house becomes the first ten rooms

[Chorus — Variation 3 — full band, congregational feel]
They weren't looking when you found them
Slow and steady, six long months
You didn't hire a team — you built a culture
And it starts with the first ten

[Outro — acoustic guitar returns, quiet]
The first ten rooms you build
Become the house

---

GENERATION NOTES:
- Keep verses intimate and conversational — don't oversing
- Chorus should feel like a room of people singing together, not a stadium
- Bridge is the emotional peak — organ should swell here, not before
- Outro should feel like the band stepping back, one guitar remaining
- Avoid: drum kit on verse 1, any synth, any bright piano
- Target: the feeling of a founding team gathered around a campfire
