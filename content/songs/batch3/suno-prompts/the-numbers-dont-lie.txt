TITLE: The Numbers Don't Lie

STYLE: Late-night jazz, Chet Baker intimacy. Muted trumpet carries the melody between vocal lines. NO acoustic guitar. Upright bass walking quarter notes, warm Rhodes piano chords, brushed snare with wire brush texture. BPM 82, key of Am. Warm male baritone, close-mic, cigarette-smoke delivery — not singing at you, singing to himself. Trumpet answers each chorus line. Bridge: trumpet solo over Rhodes and bass, achingly beautiful. Final chorus: voice and trumpet together. Smoky room ambience, 2AM bar feel. Mood: late-night reckoning, the data is the bartender who's heard it all.

SOURCE: "What is good retention?" and "What is good monthly churn?" — Lenny Rachitsky (Lenny's Newsletter)
