# Research Brief — The New Stack

## Source
**"Counterintuitive advice for building AI products"** — Lenny Rachitsky & Kyle Poyar (Newsletter, July 2, 2024)
Roundup of 20+ AI product builders including Scott Belsky, Elad Gil, Rahul Vohra, Sarah Guo, Paul Adams, Cameron Adams, Ryan J. Salva, Claire Vo, Rahul Vohra.

## Story Arc
Everyone's racing to bolt AI onto their product. 60% of companies already have, another 24% plan to. But two in five gen AI products haven't made a single dollar. The "tourists" come for the demo and leave. The counterintuitive truth from 20+ builders: the shiny model isn't the magic. UX, speed, solving real chores, prompt iteration, and old-fashioned product craft still win. The stack is new, but the work is the same.

## Key Facts & Numbers
- 60% of companies integrated gen AI; 24% more on roadmap
- 2 in 5 gen AI products: $0 revenue despite millions spent
- GitHub Copilot: 35% code suggestion acceptance rate
- 75% of incident summaries at Incident.io now AI-generated
- Shockingly low retention for most early AI apps ("tourist" problem)

## Emotional Core
The tension between the dazzle of new AI capabilities and the humbling reality that the same product fundamentals still apply. It's exciting AND grounding — the future feels warm, not cold, because it still comes down to human craft.

## THE ONE THING
The magic isn't the model — it's the same human work it's always been, just wearing a new skin.

## Songwriting Angle
Frame it as a builder sitting with their shiny new tools, realizing the real work hasn't changed. The contrast between "new stack" promise and "old craft" truth. Use imagery of building, hands, light/glow fading to reveal something solid underneath. The chorus lands on: the future looks familiar once you strip the hype away.
