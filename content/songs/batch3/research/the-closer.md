# Research Brief: The Closer

## Source
**Lessons from one of the world's top executive recruiters | Lauren Ipsen (Daversa Partners, General Catalyst)**
- Type: Podcast
- Date: 2022-11-03
- Guest: Lauren Ipsen

## Story Arc
Lauren Ipsen went from aspiring news broadcaster to one of tech's most sought-after executive recruiters. She placed 85+ senior executives across companies like Twitter, Reddit, Snapchat, Postmates, and Nextdoor. Her philosophy rejects transactional recruiting — instead, she plays a long game of genuine relationship building. The signature story: she courted a VP of Engineering (Alex Strand, who built Amazon Prime Day and Snapchat's messaging platform) for seven months before closing him in two weeks. She believed in the company so much that she followed him there four weeks later.

## Key Facts & Numbers
- 85 executives placed over her career
- Seven-month courtship → closed in two weeks (Alex Strand / IRL placement)
- She keeps calendar notes on candidates' vesting dates to time outreach
- Back channels 10 people per candidate as reference checks
- 50% networking / 50% portfolio support at General Catalyst
- Started career wanting to be on the news, pivoted to exec search

## Quotable Lines (Direct from Source)
1. "You never want to put yourself in a position where you have no idea what good looks like"
2. "People aren't just a commodity. It's so much more."
3. "Back channel references are the Instagram Reel vs. the photos you're tagged in"
4. "I have a note on my calendar that John Kim's vest just happened in March"
5. "Just because this is the best talent doesn't mean they are the best talent for this role today"
6. "A seven month game of courtship... magic can happen and you can land incredible people"
7. "You close searches and cross your fingers... he was one where I had all the confidence in the world"
8. "Once you lose trust in a candidate, it's impossible to get it back"
9. "Find excuses to continue to touch base with people that are important"
10. "I call three people. I know they're amazing. Hire one of them."

## Emotional Core
Recruiting as intimate art — patience, reading people, building trust over months before the right moment arrives. The "close" isn't a hard sell; it's the natural result of genuine connection. There's swagger in knowing your craft so well you can read a room, time a conversation, and match the right soul to the right mission.

## The ONE Thing
Great recruiting is a slow seduction — months of genuine connection that make the close feel inevitable, not transactional.
