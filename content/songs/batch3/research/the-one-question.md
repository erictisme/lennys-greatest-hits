# Research Brief: The One Question

## Source Material
- "How to learn the most about a candidate from a single interview question" (Lenny's Newsletter)
- 25 high-signal interview questions from 150+ podcast guests
- Contributors: Annie Pearl, Geoff Charles (Ramp), Yuhki Yamashita (Figma CPO), Nikhyl Singhal (Meta), Noah Weiss (Slack CPO), Shishir Mehrotra (Coda CEO), Christina Wodtke, Tom Conrad, Katie Dill (Stripe), Andrew Bosworth (Meta CTO)

## Core Insight
A single brave question — the right one — reveals more than an hour of polished answers. The best interviewers don't interrogate; they create a crack in the performance where the real person slips through. What they're all hunting for is honesty: honest about failure, honest about what's hard, honest about what they don't know.

## Key Facts Used
- Geoff Charles (Ramp): "I want to understand what hard means for them"
- Annie Pearl: "I look for people being brutally honest about how bad it was and why it failed"
- Yuhki Yamashita (Figma CPO): "Is this person authentic? Represent both sides"
- Noah Weiss (Slack CPO): "What's an unfair secret you've learned?"
- Christina Wodtke: "What question should I have asked you?" — gets honest self-disclosure
- Tom Conrad: "Imagine you had a really great day at work… what are you telling your partner?"
- Nikhyl Singhal (Meta): "What's something everyone takes for granted that you think is hogwash?" — breaks the interview mindset
- Andrew Bosworth (Meta CTO): People are rarely accurate about their critiques but usually accurate about their strengths
- The 45-minute window — precious, finite time to see the real person
- Four themes: how they handle hard stuff, how they think, how they build/ship, who they are as people

## Story Arc
An interviewer across the table, 45 minutes ticking down. The candidate is polished, prepared, performing. The interviewer has one move: the one question that bypasses the performance and lands in real territory. The moment the candidate's face changes — that's what they came for. The song lives in the space between the question and the answer.

## Emotional Core
The tension of being judged in minutes. The vulnerability of being truly seen. The quiet power of an interviewer who doesn't need to be clever — just honest. And the relief, for both parties, when the performance falls away and something real is said.

## The ONE Thing
One brave question can reveal more than an hour of polished answers — if you're willing to sit in the silence after you ask it.

## Singability Rating: 8/10
Strong interview-room setting — universally recognized tension. The phrase "tell me what hard means to you" is inherently singable: short, open vowels, memorable. The scene (two people across a table, 45 minutes) is vivid without needing explanation.

## Hook Candidates Evaluated

**A:** "One question cracks the window open / One question lets the real light in"
Pleasant but vague. "Cracks the window" is slightly cliche.

**B:** "Tell me what hard means to you / I'll know everything I need to know"
Direct, conversational, Geoff Charles-derived. Lands on first listen.

**C:** "You practiced all the answers / But I only have the one"
Strong ironic tension — the interviewer's quiet confidence. Slightly cryptic on first listen but earns repeated play.

**D:** "The question no one asks / Is the only one that counts"
Too meta and abstract. Doesn't give us a scene.

**Chosen Hook:** "Tell me what hard means to you" as the killer line. Setup built around the 45-minute window and the performance that precedes the question.

## Emotional Direction
Indie folk-pop. Stephen Day / Hollow Coves energy: warm, earnest, building quietly. The song should feel like a quiet room where something true is about to happen. BPM 104, Key G major. Acoustic guitar fingerpicking, light percussion entering verse 2, gentle swell in the chorus. Close-mic warm male vocal — earnest, not clever.
