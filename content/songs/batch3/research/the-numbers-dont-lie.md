# Research Brief: The Numbers Don't Lie

## Source Material
- "What is good retention?" (Lenny's Newsletter)
- "What is good monthly churn?" (Lenny's Newsletter)
- Survey of 20 growth experts on retention benchmarks
- ProfitWell data from 13,000 SaaS companies (Patrick Campbell)

## Core Insight
Retention is the truth-teller. The retention curve either flattens (product-market fit) or keeps falling (slow death). No pitch deck overrides it. No growth hack survives it. The numbers don't lie — founders do.

## Key Facts Used
- 8% monthly churn = two-thirds of users gone per year
- 4% monthly churn = rebuilding a third of your user base annually
- Consumer social: ~25% 6-month retention = good, ~45% = great
- Enterprise SaaS: ~70% = good, ~90% = great
- The moment the curve flattens = the signal of product-market fit

## Story Arc
A founder alone at 3am, dashboard open, watching the line curve downward. They've heard the advice, done the math, built the features — but the curve keeps falling. The song walks through denial, rationalization, and finally honest reckoning: the numbers knew the truth before they did. The retention curve doesn't care about their narrative. It just tells what happened.

## Emotional Core
That specific anxiety of watching a graph move in the wrong direction — and knowing it's telling you something you don't want to hear. The bittersweet relief of finally accepting what the data says. Data as prophecy. Data as the most honest thing in the room.

## The ONE Thing
Your retention curve is the only honest voice in the building. It can't be argued with, spun, or fired. It just shows you the truth.

## Singability Rating: 9/10
Concrete numbers, dramatic tension, universal "truth you can't escape" theme. The 3am setting is immediately evocative. "They don't come back" is instantly singable and emotionally complete.

## Hook Candidates Evaluated

**A:** "The curve don't lie / Two-thirds gone by December / The curve don't lie"
Good number, strong. But "curve don't lie" is slightly cliche.

**B:** "They don't come back / And the numbers knew before you / They don't come back"
Haunting, personal, universal. Data-as-prophecy. Winner.

**C:** "If they don't return / Nothing else you built will matter / If they don't return"
Brian Balfour paraphrase. Strong but slightly preachy.

**Chosen Hook:** Candidate B — "They don't come back"
