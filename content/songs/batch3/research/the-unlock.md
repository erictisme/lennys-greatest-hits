# Research Brief — "The Unlock"
Album: By the Numbers
Source: "How to determine your activation metric" + "What is a good activation rate" (Lenny's Newsletter / Hila Qu)

## The Core Insight
There is one specific moment — early in a user's journey — that predicts whether they will stay forever or leave within a week. It's not signup. It's not a purchase. It's a discrete action that correlates so strongly with long-term retention that once you find it, everything else in growth snaps into focus.

## Story Arc
Builder staring at dashboards full of noise → narrowing the search → testing actions against retention curves → the eureka: one moment predicts everything → now you build toward that moment, obsessively.

## Emotional Core
The thrill of narrowing — of all the noise in a product, finding the one signal that actually matters. It's a detective story. The satisfaction isn't the metric itself; it's the moment you realize you've been measuring the wrong thing your whole career.

## Key Source Details
- Facebook's activation: 7 friends in 10 days (the classic)
- Slack's activation: team of 2+ users sends 50+ messages in 7 days
- Figma: two people collaborating in the same file within 24 hours
- Snyk "F30D": fix a vulnerability within 30 days
- Linear: deliberately doesn't measure activation (Karri Saarinen — contrarian)
- Average activation rate across 500+ companies: 34% (median: 25%)
- Best metric = highly predictive (2x retention) AND highly actionable
- The aha moment = habit formation, not just value perception
- Hila Qu: "This reveals the deep, deep level of their thinking"
- Process: brainstorm candidates → regression on retention → experiments for causality

## The ONE Thing
There's a single moment in your user's journey that predicts everything — and you almost certainly don't know what it is yet.

## Quotable Lines (source material)

## Song Title
"The Unlock" — the activation event as a literal unlocking of the user's future. Also plays on the discovery feeling: the metric is a key, and once you find it, a door opens.

## Genre Direction
Dream-pop / lo-fi — Doris Club / Tom Misch energy. Playful, curious, unhurried. The song should feel like solving a puzzle in a quiet room, not sprinting to a finish line. BPM 96, Key C major.
