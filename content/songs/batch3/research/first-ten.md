# Research Brief — "First Ten"
Album: The Talent Search
Source: "Hiring your early team" + "Winning at early-stage hiring" (Lenny's Newsletter)

## The Core Insight
Your first ten hires don't just do jobs — they become the company's immune system. They define what you reward, what you tolerate, how you fight, and how you celebrate. Every person you hire after them will be filtered through a culture those ten people created. Choose wrong and you spend years trying to undo the default you accidentally set.

## Story Arc
An empty room. A founder who nobody wants to bet on yet. The slow, patient work of finding one person who believes — then another. The best ones weren't looking. You had to earn them over months. Then suddenly, great people are pulling in other great people, and the compound effect kicks in. The first ten rooms you build become the house.

## Emotional Core
The loneliness of building before anyone believes in you. The audacity of the ask — "come risk your career on this." The slow courtship of someone who doesn't need you. The breakthrough when a person says yes not because you paid them more, but because your vision got into them. Then the snowball: great people want to work with great people, and suddenly you have something real.

## Key Source Details
- 2/3 of early-stage companies hired an engineer first — developers, developers, developers
- Segment co-founder: "little appetite to join four crazy guys in a room who had been failing for two years straight"
- Vanta (Christina Cacioppo): hired a compliance expert first — "we could build, but we wanted a check on whether we were building the right thing"
- Coda hired a recruiter (Kenny) as an early hire — "people like Kenny don't come along often"
- Sam Corcos (Levels): "Be prepared to commit ~100 hours for each hire"
- "Our best hires are people who weren't looking — we slowly won their interest over ~6 months"
- Siqi Chen: "You know you're pitching candidates well enough when they offer to invest"
- Nick Handel: "Never lower your recruiting bar — it will have longer-term negative consequences"
- Stytch: "We do a Zoom to surprise the candidate with everyone from their interview panel"
- 5 themes of hiring success: Captivating vision, A++ early team, Putting in the work, Network, Pitching prowess
- "Great people want to work with great people" — the snowball effect

## The ONE Thing
Your best hires weren't looking — and that's exactly why you had to find them.

## Song Title
"First Ten" — the first ten hires are a specific, countable thing. Also echoes "first step" and "listen" (hidden in the sounds). The number gives it weight: not "early team" (vague) but ten specific people who became your foundation.

## Genre Direction
Liturgical folk / hymn-style — CityAlight energy. Community, shared mission, building together. Warm and congregational. BPM 92, Key D major. Acoustic guitar, light organ, gentle build to full band. Should feel like a founding anthem — the kind of song a startup team would play on the day they finally feel like a real company.

## Killer Line
"They weren't looking when you found them"
