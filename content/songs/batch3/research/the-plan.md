# Research Brief: The Plan

## Source
- **Article:** "The Secret to a Great Planning Process — Lessons from Airbnb and Eventbrite" (First Round Review / Lenny's Newsletter, 2019-09-26)
- **Supporting:** "How Gong builds product" (2024-01-23), Farhan Thawar podcast (2024-12-19)

## Story Arc
Lenny and co-author Nels Gilbreth spent a combined 17 years at Airbnb (7 years) and Eventbrite (10 years), living through dozens of planning cycles. They saw the worst: annual plans still unfinished three months into the year, half-assed plans rushed through and abandoned after six months, bruised egos, misalignment. But they also saw one team that consistently stayed ahead — ready to roll on day one of every quarter, delivering more impact with higher morale. The root cause of bad planning is simple: nobody knows who does what, when. Their answer: the W Framework — four steps that bounce between leadership and teams like a letter W. Context down, Plans up, Integration down, Buy-in up.

## Key Facts & Numbers

## Emotional Core
The dread of planning season. Everyone's been through it — the chaos, the politics, the feeling that you're building sandcastles while the tide comes in. But when it works, when everyone knows their part and the W bounces cleanly, there's this quiet confidence. You're ready on day one. You're aligned. The plan isn't a document — it's the feeling that everyone's rowing in the same direction.

## THE ONE THING
Great planning isn't about the plan — it's about everyone knowing their role in making it.
