# Research Brief: The Call

## Source
- **Article:** "My favorite decision-making frameworks" (Lenny's Newsletter, 2024-02-06)
- **Supporting:** Gokul Rajaram podcast (2022-06-13), Shreyas Doshi Live (2024-10-31)

## Story Arc
Companies live and die by decision quality. But most teams get stuck in consensus loops — endless meetings, head-banging debates, nobody owning the outcome. Gokul Rajaram built SPADE at Square after employees named "decision-making" their #1 frustration. The insight: consensus means no ownership. What matters isn't that everyone agrees — it's that everyone is heard, and then ONE person decides and owns it. The emotional core: that lonely moment when the room looks at you and it's YOUR call.

## Key Facts & Numbers
- Square surveyed employees: #1 frustration was decision-making
- SPADE: Setting, People, Alternatives, Decide, Explain
- Bezos one-way door vs. two-way door decisions
- Coinbase framework can run start-to-finish in under 15 minutes
- Brian Armstrong: attributes of good decisions — speed, quality, buy-in

## Emotional Core
The weight of being the one who has to decide. Not the framework itself — the human moment. The room goes quiet. Everyone's been heard. Now someone has to choose, knowing they'll own the outcome either way. It's lonely. It's necessary. It's leadership.

## THE ONE THING
Someone has to make the call — and that someone has to live with it.
