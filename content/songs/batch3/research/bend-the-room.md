# Research Brief: Bend the Room

## Sources
- "A PM's guide to influence" by Jules Walter (newsletter, 2024-10-01)
- "How to get better at influence" by Lenny Rachitsky (newsletter, 2022-09-20)

## Story Arc
Jules Walter built his reputation as someone who could "bend people to alignment" across YouTube, Slack, and Google Gemini. At Slack, he needed CEO Stewart Butterfield to approve a controversial monetization experiment. Butterfield was intensely user-driven and hated anything that felt like extracting value. So Jules reframed the entire pitch around user benefit — "Paid users find more value and retain better. Yet we make it hard for people to discover that Slack has a paid version." The CEO opened up. The experiment drove a 20% increase in paying teams. At YouTube, Jules needed a partner team to prioritize his feature. He gathered intel through 1:1s, learned their OKRs, walked them through his forecast model, even recommended what they could deprioritize. At Google, he ran 14+ stakeholder meetings before a strategy summit — converting detractors, enlisting champions — and his plan was voted top 3 across all of YouTube.

Lenny frames influence through Frodo Baggins — the most unassuming character trusted with the most important mission. The 7 strategies: make their goals your goals, charge your trust battery (Tobi Lutke), help them see what you see, show success, bring evidence, leverage authority, be likable. The Airbnb Superhost story: Lenny reframed from host retention to guest conversion and the search team finally agreed.

## Emotional Core
The quiet confidence of someone who walks into a room full of disagreement and walks out with everyone aligned — not through force but through understanding what everyone already wants. The protagonist isn't the loudest voice; they're the one who listened first.

## THE ONE THING
Influence is reading the room so well that your ask becomes everyone else's idea.
