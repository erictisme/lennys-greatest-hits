Album: By the Numbers
Song Number: 3

## Key Facts & Quotes Extracted

**The core tension:**
- Olga Berezovsky: "retention is both the most important and the least understood metric"
- Brian Balfour: "if you have poor retention, nothing else matters"
- SQL complexity: "it takes up to six months to nail accurate retention reporting" — the thing everyone says matters most is also the hardest thing to measure correctly

**The analyst's experience:**
- Watching a cohort retention chart week by week — color-coded grid, every column a week, every row a cohort
- The curve that keeps falling: normal, natural, devastating to watch
- The curve that finally flattens: that's your signal. That's PMF.
- Even flattening at 10% is better than never flattening at all

**The hidden cost of bad definitions:**
- "Active" can mean: visits, sessions, logins, main user action — each has pitfalls
- You can spend months thinking you have retention when you're measuring the wrong thing
- Change.org monitoring Day 7 and Day 30 retention led to 450% growth in petition starters per day

**The strategies (story material, not stat dumps):**
- Andrew Chen: "The real levers to improve retention are in the experience for new users"
- Casey Winters: "Get people to the core product as fast as possible — but not faster"
- Scott Belsky: "Users don't want to make choices in the first mile"
- Airbnb: offering "pause" instead of "cancel" reduced host churn — the save that wasn't a save
- 7 strategies: improve product, improve onboarding, make it stickier, catch before they leave, remind of value, bring back after gone, change your users

**The two truth lenses:**
- X-day retention (did they come back on day N?) vs unbounded retention (do they ever come back?)
- Both reveal something real. Neither is the whole truth.

## Story Arc
An analyst staring at the retention curve — watching it fall week after week, questioning everything they thought "active" meant. The breakthrough comes not from a clever insight but from patience: waiting long enough to see whether the curve flattens. The emotional payoff is quiet and counterintuitive: a flat line is the best news you can get.

## Emotional Core
Patience as a skill. The anxiety of a falling curve. The discipline of defining things correctly before you build. The quiet triumph when the curve finally holds.

## The ONE Thing
Retention hides its truth in time. The only way to read it is to wait — and to make sure you know what you're measuring before the clock starts.

## Killer Line Candidate
"A flat line means someone stayed" — counterintuitive (flat line = bad in most contexts), jazz-cool delivery, captures the entire insight without explanation.

## Runner-Up
"You're not watching numbers — you're watching people leave" — visceral, shifts the frame from analytics to humanity.
