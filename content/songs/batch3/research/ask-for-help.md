# Research Brief: Ask for Help

## Source
**Newsletter:** "On asking for help (even when you really don't want to)"
**Author:** Natalie Rothfels (Newsletter Fellow guest post)
**Date:** 2024-08-06

## Story Arc
Natalie Rothfels, executive coach and former PM at Khan Academy and Quizlet, hit burnout in late 2021. Made it a goal to ask for help once a week. The central character study is "Meena" — a Group PM with 10 years experience at a 300-person Series C startup. Meena was drowning: managing PMs, doing IC work, supporting hiring. Her "real" work didn't start until after 5 PM. She refused to share half-baked ideas out of fear of looking incompetent. She'd sit at a blank screen while her inner critic said "This is your job. You're so bad at this." The irony: she was actually great at her job.

The transformation: Natalie helped Meena navigate her fear (it lived in her chest, constricting up her neck), make small asks to peers first, then bigger asks to her manager. Result: Meena's stress dropped, relationships became reciprocal, and she was asked to expand leadership to two more teams — sustainably.

## Key Concepts & Quotes

1. **"Asking for help is a sign of weakness, and weakness is a sign of death."** — A client's raw expression of the fear.
2. **"The exhaustion of subconsciously assuming I have to do everything on my own robbed me of agency, joy, and so much energy."** — Meena
3. **"You're slowing your trajectory if you don't build this muscle."** — Lenny/Natalie
4. **6 fears of asking:**
   - Fear of being a burden
   - Fear of appearing incompetent
   - Fear of rejection
   - Fear of losing control
   - Fear of uncertainty
   - Fear of the cost (vulnerability for nothing)
5. **Meena's fear lived in her chest** — constriction up her neck, making it hard to speak.
6. **The template:** Signpost → Clear request → Rationale → Why them → Timeline → Opt-in
7. **"I worked 10 times harder to avoid the vulnerability of asking."** — Natalie
8. **5 types of help:** Perspective, Information, Task progression, Empathetic support, Advocacy
9. **"I can sleep at night without the burden of chronic overwhelm and rumination."** — Natalie after learning to ask
10. **The blank screen scene:** Meena sitting for hours, inner critic on repeat, while her team got all her energy.

## Emotional Core
Pride disguised as strength. The person who handles everything, helps everyone, answers every Slack — but won't let anyone see them struggle. The song should feel like the moment the armor comes off and you finally say the words: "I need help."

## THE ONE THING
The strongest thing you can do is admit you can't do it alone.
