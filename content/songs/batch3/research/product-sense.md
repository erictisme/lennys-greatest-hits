# Research Brief: Product Sense

## Source
- **Article:** "The definitive guide to mastering product sense interviews" by Ben Erez (via Lenny's Newsletter)
- **Date:** April 1, 2025
- **Word count:** 9,102

## Story Arc
Ben Erez spent four intense weeks preparing for a Facebook PM interview after a decade in startups. He watched every mock interview on YouTube, drafted templates from patterns he spotted, and landed the offer. Then he sat on the other side — interviewing 50+ candidates — and saw the gap between people who could recite frameworks and people who genuinely understood users. He went on to coach hundreds of candidates, distilling the product sense interview into five dimensions: clear communication, product motivation, segmentation, problem identification, and solution development. The core insight: product sense isn't about memorizing structures. It's about seeing the human behind the product.

## Key Facts & Numbers
- PS interviews are typically 45 minutes (35 min for the core exercise)
- Ben interviewed 50+ candidates at Facebook
- Coached hundreds of PM candidates
- Candidates have landed comp packages upward of $600K using his frameworks
- Adopted by Facebook, Google, Stripe, OpenAI, Block, and many others

## Quotable Lines

7. "Excellence in one area can't compensate for weakness in another"

## Emotional Core
The anxiety of sitting in an interview knowing you can't fake intuition. The moment when you stop thinking about frameworks and start thinking about people. Product sense is empathy dressed up as a professional skill.

## THE ONE THING
Product sense isn't a test you pass — it's a way of seeing people that you either practice daily or you don't.
