# Research Brief: "High Intensity"

**Song concept:** Shopify's high-intensity engineering culture — killing meetings, building things, choosing the hard path, and doing more per minute instead of more hours.

**Sources:**
- Farhan Thawar, VP & Head of Engineering at Shopify — Lenny's Podcast, December 19, 2024
- Tobi Lütke, CEO of Shopify — Lenny's Podcast, February 2, 2025

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** The world defaults to low-intensity work. Twelve-hour days of foosball, coffee breaks, Slack pings, endless recurring meetings. Busyness masquerades as output. Everyone expands work to fill the time available. Parkinson's Law is quietly winning.

**Turning point:** Farhan joins Shopify — already messaging Tobi about "Meeting Armageddon" before he even starts. He brings a simple, radical belief: one hour is one hour. You don't need more hours. You need more kilojoules per hour. Instead of 10 resumes a day, build something. Instead of planning a reorg for six months, launch it in two weeks and move on. Delete the meetings. Delete the code that shouldn't exist. Delete what doesn't serve the work.

**End:** A 10,000+ person remote company hitting record GMV, blowing past earnings calls, hiring 1,000 interns, with individual contributors in only 3 hours of meetings per week. The intensity isn't burnout — it's the opposite. It's deep, focused craft. A crafter's paradise. The hard path was always the faster one.

---

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- Shopify has 10,000–11,000 employees, fully remote
- Individual contributors spend ~3 hours/week in meetings (down 50–60% from ~5–6 hours before Meeting Armageddon)
- Managers spend ~6–7 hours/week in meetings
- Six-week review cycles with Tobi for every project across the company
- GSD (Get Shit Done) = weekly updates to the whole company on every project
- Delete Code Club finds 1 million+ lines of code to delete at every hack day
- GitHub Copilot wrote 1 million lines of code for Shopify — Farhan's response: "Where's the delete?"
- Shopify pair programs 4–8 hours per week per engineer (vs. 40 hours at Xtreme Labs)
- Tobi & CTO Cody used to pair program with a timer: if not done in 1 hour, delete all code and restart
- Meeting Armageddon: once a year, all recurring meetings with 3+ internal people are deleted. 2-week moratorium on new recurring meetings.
- Farhan had 120 direct reports at Xtreme Labs with zero scheduled one-on-ones
- His first React Native decision in week 1 at Shopify burned ~100 engineers for 18 months
- Shopify is hiring 1,000 interns in 2025
- Tobi has been coding since age 16, did his apprenticeship in Germany
- Shopify went remote-first after COVID, growing from Ottawa to global talent pool

## Emotional Core

**The human tension:** Everyone wants to do great work. But most companies build invisible systems that make great work nearly impossible — endless meetings, sprawling codebases, Slack pings, status reports. Workers feel productive but are spinning. Farhan's insight is almost loving in its implication: you are capable of so much more in the same hours. The intensity isn't punishment — it's respect. We believe in you enough to remove all the friction and let you actually build.

**Counterintuitive truth:** Less meetings = more intensity. Fewer features = faster velocity. Deleting code = more power. Pair programming (two people, one computer) = more output. Choosing the hard path = the easier life.

**The feeling:** Rebellious joy. The pleasure of defying conventional wisdom and being right. The pride of craft. The satisfaction of shipping something real.

---

## The ONE Thing

**One sentence:** Do more per minute, not more hours — and the fastest way to do that is to ruthlessly delete everything that isn't the work.

## Emotional Direction

**Mood:** Defiant, energized, slightly punk — but grounded in craft not chaos. This is not anger, it's *clarity*. The satisfaction of someone who has figured something out and can't wait to show you.

**Energy (1–10):** 8.5 — High energy, driving momentum, but with moments of stillness when the craft takes over (pair programming section should feel almost meditative in contrast to the meeting-killing verses)

**Genre suggestion:** Indie rock / post-punk with clean production — think early Vampire Weekend meets LCD Soundsystem. Driving rhythmic guitar, snappy drums, crisp vocals. Optional: a bridge that drops to just voice + minimal instrumentation when describing pair programming ("two people, one machine"), then builds back.

*Why this genre:* The Shopify culture is rhythmic, iterative, and slightly countercultural — it has the same "we figured out something everyone else missed" energy as early indie rock. LCD Soundsystem specifically because it sounds like smart people making dance music — intellectual and physical at the same time. This needs to feel like something you'd hear at a Shopify hack day, not a corporate motivational video.

**Tempo:** 120–130 BPM. Urgent but not frantic.

## Creative Direction

**Concept:** The song is an invitation to defect from low-intensity culture. Each verse names a specific Shopify practice (Meeting Armageddon, pair programming, Delete Code Club, choose the hard path) as its own small rebellion. The chorus lands the thesis: intensity isn't hours, it's presence per hour.

**Voice:** First person plural — "we." The voice of someone inside Shopify explaining the culture, but also any engineer or builder who has ever felt their company's systems were working against them. Universal enough to resonate, specific enough to feel real.

**Narrative arc across verses:**
- **Verse 1:** The old world — foosball at noon, 12-hour days, endless Slack. "I spent an hour in a room with no decision / another morning lost to someone else's vision"
- **Pre-chorus:** The turn — the insight that one hour is one hour, but what you do with it is everything
- **Chorus:** The declaration — Meeting Armageddon / more per minute / build something
- **Verse 2:** The Shopify practice in detail — pair programming, two people one machine, deleting code, GSD weekly updates, six-week reviews
- **Bridge:** "Why don't you look at the API docs / and build something?" — the advice to the outside world / job seekers
- **Outro:** Quiet, earned — the crafter's paradise, the trust battery full, Tobi coding at 1am with the engineers

**Avoid:** Corporate cheerleading, buzzwords without specificity, anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post. Every line should feel like it was written by someone who has actually seen Meeting Armageddon happen.

**Key images to hit:**
- Deleting a million lines of code
- Two keyboards, one machine
- The recurring meeting that disappears
- Resumes vs. building something real
- The six-week review where Tobi asks "why is this taking so long?"
- "Crafters' paradise"
