# Research Brief: The Value Loop

**Source:** "The Subscription Value Loop: A framework for growing consumer subscription businesses"
**Author:** Phil Carter (guest post on Lenny's Newsletter)
**Date:** September 3, 2024
**Type:** Newsletter
**Tags:** b2c, growth, pricing, startups

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning — The seductive trap:**
Consumer subscription apps are deceptively easy to launch. No sales team. High margins. Global distribution through app stores. Anyone can get in. Hundreds of thousands have. That's exactly the problem — the ease of entry creates fierce competition, channel saturation, and subscription fatigue. Meanwhile, Apple's ATT restrictions gutted paid acquisition. The model that looked simple to scale became a treadmill: keep running just to stay alive.

**Turning Point — The framework reveals the game:**
Phil Carter analyzed 30,000+ subscription apps (290M subscribers via RevenueCat) and found a stark truth: even top-quartile apps only convert 1 in 20 installs into a paid subscriber. They lose more than half their annual subscribers after year one. Fewer than 50 consumer subscription apps have ever reached $1B+ valuations. Fewer than 10 have $10B+ market caps. The ones that make it aren't just better at marketing — they've built a compounding loop: Value Creation → Value Delivery → Value Capture → reinvest → repeat.

**End — The loop or the grind:**
The winners run a self-reinforcing machine. Every dollar retained funds better acquisition. Better acquisition funds better product. Better product feeds retention. The loop compounds. The losers pay for installs, watch them churn, pay again. Two companies, same category. One loops. One grinds. The loop always wins.

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- **30,000+ subscription apps** analyzed via RevenueCat proprietary data
- **290 million subscribers** in the dataset
- **1 in 20 installs** converts to paid (even top-quartile apps)
- **>50% of annual subscribers** lost after the first year
- **>50% of monthly subscribers** lost after just 3 months
- **Fewer than 50** consumer subscription apps have ever reached $1B+ valuation
- **Fewer than 10** are publicly traded at $10B+ market cap
- Pricing optimization consistently generates **5%–15% lift** on subscription revenue
- AllTrails raised price from **$29.99 → $35.99**, drove **~8.3% ARPU lift**
- **>75% of trial starts** occur on the same day a user installs an app (often within minutes)
- Day 1 Paywall View Rate target: **at or above 80%**
- Blinkist timeline paywall: **+23% trial start rate, -4% cancellations, +9% subscriber conversion**
- **20%–40% of churn** is involuntary (expired cards, failed payments)
- Web flows can boost LTV by **30%–50%** vs. app store subscriptions (Palta/Flo data)
- Duolingo grew DAUs **4.5x from 2018 to 2022** by focusing on retention (CURR)
- Flo gets **>50% of MAUs and revenue** from users who installed over a year ago
- Ladder grew from **9,000 to 50,000+ subscribers** in 2023 using web-first TikTok funnel
- Superhuman PMF Score went from **22% → 58%** in three quarters by targeting the right users
- ChatGPT reached **100M MAUs in 2 months**, largely through organic growth

## Emotional Core

**The human tension:** The subscription model promises passive, compounding revenue — the dream of "build it and it pays you forever." But the reality is a constant leak. Users churn. Payments fail. Apple takes 30%. The competition copies you. Every month is a fight to keep what you earned last month. The song is about the seduction of the loop — and the exhaustion of the grind when you haven't built one.

**The feeling:** That specific dread of a SaaS founder watching their churn dashboard on the first of the month. But also the quiet confidence of someone who finally built a product so good that the loop just... runs. The contrast between "running on a treadmill" and "running downhill with the wind at your back."

---

## The ONE Thing

> If your product doesn't create, deliver, and capture value in a self-reinforcing loop, you're just renting growth — and the bill comes due every month.

- Neo-soul adds warmth that keeps the data from feeling cold — this isn't a lecture, it's someone who *cares* about this problem.

## Creative Direction

**Song Structure — Mirror the Loop:**

The structure itself should reinforce the framework. The song never "ends" — it loops back.

```
[Intro] — The seductive promise: "anyone can launch"
[Verse 1] — Value Creation: getting the user to the moment of value
[Pre-Chorus] — The moment of truth: trial starts, the clock is ticking
[Chorus A] — The loop turns (trial → convert → retain → grow)
[Verse 2] — Value Delivery: how you get them in the door (word of mouth, web, TikTok)
[Pre-Chorus] — The paywall moment, 80% should see it
[Chorus B] — Same melody, new urgency: the loop OR the grind
[Bridge] — The stats hit hard: "fewer than fifty," "one in twenty"
[Chorus — reprise] — Now it sounds like resolution, not just repetition
[Outro] — The groove fades but doesn't stop. The loop keeps running.
```

**Rhythmic note:** Consider a drum pattern that mimics a recurring cycle — a hi-hat pattern that never drops, like a clock or a heartbeat. The bass should walk in a loop. The melody resolves but the groove doesn't.

**Key lyric constraint:** Every chorus must contain at least one concrete number. Numbers are the hook. "One in twenty" is more memorable than "low conversion rates."

**Thematic thread:** Use water/tide imagery — "the leak," "the drain," "the tide turns" — alongside machine imagery — "the loop," "the engine," "the flywheel." The tension between leak and loop is the emotional core.
