# Research Brief: The Hiring Playbook

**Song concept:** Gokul Rajaram's tactical wisdom for hiring leaders
**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — "Gokul Rajaram on designing your product development process, when and how to hire your first PM, a playbook for hiring leaders" (June 13, 2022)
**Guest:** Gokul Rajaram (DoorDash, Square, Google, Facebook; board member at Coinbase, Pinterest, Trade Desk)

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning — The Puzzle:**
A CEO messages Gokul in the morning: "How did you know this person was available at that company?" Three hires in a row, all senior leaders, all sourced from Gokul's advice. The CEO is baffled. Gokul realizes he has been running an unconscious playbook: he never recommends a person — he recommends a company. "I didn't know about this person. I just knew that company was really good at sales."

**Turning Point — The Framework Clicks:**
Gokul maps it out explicitly for the first time. Find the best-in-class company for that function. Not your competitor — a company serving the same customer type. Then don't poach the head. Go one level down: hire the lieutenant. Or two levels down: hire the lieutenant's lieutenant. Square went public as one of the only public fintechs in 2015, and Gokul watched their teams get raided exactly this way by other fintechs. He was on the receiving end. Now he weaponizes it in reverse.

**End — The Complete Playbook:**
The playbook has three legs:
1. Target best-in-class companies in adjacent (not competing) spaces
2. Hire the lieutenant — not the head
3. Spend two hours a day on hiring (one hour reach-outs, one hour meetings) and do it every day for 30 days = 60 candidate conversations

Supporting wisdom: delay VP and Director titles as long as possible (Square's "head of" culture meant zero title conflict); build your personal brand online so your LinkedIn isn't the first thing people find when they Google you.

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- Gokul has worked at Google (2003), Facebook, Square, DoorDash — board at Coinbase, Pinterest, Trade Desk
- Square went public in **2015** — one of the very few public fintechs at the time
- Post-IPO, Square's teams in compliance, risk, and payments were systematically raided by other fintechs
- **2 hours a day** on hiring: 1 hour outreach, 1 hour meetings (two 30-min conversations)
- Over **30 days** that equals **60 candidate conversations** and 30 hours of LinkedIn outreach
- Gokul's Square title: "Caviar Lead" — Jack Dorsey introduced flat "head of" / "lead" nomenclature
- Started angel investing in **2007** — 15 years of investing as of the podcast
- Adam Zamos (head of people at Square): "We used to go hire the up-and-coming lieutenants of the lieutenants"
- Gokul's rule of thumb: organic traffic should be **40-50%** of new customer acquisition
- Google AdSense origin story: Gokul became a part-time PM nights-and-weekends for a Sergey Brin project → grew into AdSense within 3 months

## Emotional Core

**Human Tension:** Everyone is asking "who should I hire?" when they should be asking "where do I find the right person?" The playbook flips the question. It's the difference between fishing with a pole and fishing with a net cast at the right pond.

**Feeling:** Calm, practical confidence. This is wisdom that feels almost too simple — like someone just told you the answer to the puzzle you've been circling for years. The emotional note is: *relief + clarity*. A mentor handing you the keys.

**Deeper tension:** Hiring is the thing that keeps every founder up at night. It's also the thing most founders underspend time on, or approach reactively. Gokul's gift is converting anxiety into a repeatable system. The song should carry that shift: from anxious to systematic, from reactive to strategic.

## Emotional Direction

**Mood:** Warm, tactical, mentor-like. Think of an older colleague walking you through a whiteboard session. Not cold or corporate — wise and generous.

**Energy:** 5/10 — mid-tempo, conversational groove. Not urgent, not sleepy. The pace of someone who knows what they're doing and has time to explain.

**Genre Suggestion:** Neo-soul / acoustic R&B with a hint of lo-fi hip hop texture.

**Why this genre fits:**
- Neo-soul has that "wisdom passed down" feeling — think D'Angelo or Erykah Badu explaining life in song
- The lyrical content is tactical and specific (names, numbers, steps) which fits over a relaxed groove — the contrast between busy-brain content and chill delivery creates pleasant tension
- Lo-fi texture signals "late night office" or "mentor conversation in a coffee shop" which is exactly Gokul's energy
- Avoid anything aggressive or anthemic — this is not a battle cry, it's a quiet revelation
- Reference artists from Eric's palette: similar vibe to Amos Lee acoustic warmth + Rex Orange County playfulness

**Tempo:** ~85-95 BPM. Room for the lyrical ideas to breathe without dragging.

## Creative Direction

**Tone:** Practical and warm. This song should feel like Gokul is sitting across from you, leaning forward, saying "here's what nobody tells you." No ego. Mentor energy.

**Narrative voice:** First person Gokul, or third person observational (Lenny witnessing). Could also be second person ("you"), giving advice directly to the listener.

**Structural suggestion:**
- Verse 1: The problem — everyone asks "who" instead of "where" (set up the wrong-question trap)
- Pre-chorus: The morning text from the CEO — three hires in a row, how did you know?
- Chorus: The inversion — find the company, find the lieutenant
- Verse 2: Square goes public, teams get raided, Gokul watches and learns
- Bridge: The daily discipline — two hours, sixty people, calendar is your compass
- Outro: The bigger truth — titles don't matter, scope matters; build your brand, wait for serendipity

**Imagery to use:** LinkedIn org charts, calendar blocks, morning Slack messages, the moment a company rings the IPO bell and suddenly everyone wants a piece of the team, whiteboard org charts, the "lieutenant" as a chess piece.

**What to avoid:** Generic hiring language ("find the right culture fit," "top talent," etc.). Every line should be traceable back to something Gokul specifically said. The power is in the specificity.
