TITLE: Three Eras

STYLE: Indie folk alt-country ballad, 3/4 waltz, 76 BPM, Db minor. Warm storytelling male vocal — intimate and close-mic'd in verse 1, heavier and strained by verse 2, quiet and resolved in verse 3. Fingerpicked acoustic guitar opens alone. Violin enters at verse 2, sparse and melancholic, builds through verse 3. Piano enters at the bridge, just single notes under the vocal. Full band — acoustic guitar, violin, piano, brushed drums, upright bass — swells into the final chorus. The violin is the emotional thread: it carries the weight of everything that came before. Sonic reference: Bon Iver atmospheric folk production, The National storytelling weight, Hollow Coves build and release. Strip to voice and guitar for the bridge. No electric guitars. No distortion. Bittersweet and cinematic.

SOURCE: Drew Houston (Lenny's Podcast)
