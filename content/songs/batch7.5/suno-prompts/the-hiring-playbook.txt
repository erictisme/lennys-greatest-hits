TITLE: The Hiring Playbook

STYLE: Neo-soul acoustic R&B, Bb major, 88 BPM, 4/4 time. Warm male baritone vocal, intimate and conversational — Leon Bridges warmth meets Tom Misch groove meets Chet Baker cool. Defining texture: muted trumpet as a recurring melodic voice between vocal phrases, soft and smoky. Rhodes piano carrying the harmonic foundation with gentle chord stabs. Upright bass walking a lazy two-feel. Finger snaps on beats 2 and 4. Brushed snare. No electric guitar. No aggressive percussion. Late-night coffee shop energy — wise, unhurried, mentor-like. Vocal delivery: reflective and assured, never urgent. Song should feel like someone leaning across a table and handing you the answer.

SOURCE: Gokul Rajaram (Lenny's Podcast)
