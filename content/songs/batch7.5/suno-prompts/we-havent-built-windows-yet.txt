TITLE: We Haven't Built Windows Yet

STYLE: Chamber pop jazz-folk, Ab major, 72 BPM, 6/8 time. Warm male vocal — breathy, awestruck, intimate delivery. Jazz grand piano as the sole harmonic foundation (NO acoustic guitar). Light brushed jazz drums enter at verse 2. Violin and cello swell gently at chorus, pulling back for bridge. Space is essential — leave room between phrases. Sufjan Stevens spaciousness, early Bon Iver intimacy, Chet Baker cool restraint. Piano voicings should be open jazz chords (maj7, 9ths). No electric instruments. No distortion. The arrangement should feel like standing inside something too large to see the edges of — quiet wonder, not triumph.

SOURCE: Nick Turley (Lenny's Podcast)

---
