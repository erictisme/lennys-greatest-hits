TITLE: The Hard Thing

STYLE: Dark acoustic folk, Americana, G minor, 82 BPM, 4/4 time. Male vocal, low register — intimate and confessional in verses, gritty and strained on emotional peaks. Low cello drone underneath everything, the weight that never lifts. Acoustic guitar sparse and deliberate, single notes not strummed. Room reverb on vocals and guitar — sounds like a late-night living room, not a studio. No drums in verses or pre-chorus. Minimal kick drum and brush snare enter on chorus only, then disappear again. Cello swells slightly on bridge. Silence is a feature — leave space between lines. Influences: The National's bruised delivery, Bon Iver's rawness, Nick Drake's darkness. NOT anthemic, NOT inspirational rock, NOT cinematic swells. This should feel like someone telling the truth at last call.

SOURCE: Ben Horowitz (Lenny's Podcast)
