# Radical Candor

**Album:** The Weight
**Source:** Kim Scott on Lenny's Podcast (2023-12-10)
**The ONE Thing:** Silence isn't kindness — the words you won't say are the cruelest thing you'll do to someone you love.

---

[Verse 1, gentle]
He'd bring the right candy at the right time

## Craft Notes

**Killer Line:** "All my quiet kindness was the cruelest thing he'd heard"

**Syllable Check:**
- Verse lines: 9-11 syllables, consistent
- Chorus lines: 6-9 syllables, shorter than verse
- Bridge lines: 9-12 syllables, emotional expansion justified

**End-Stopped Check:** Every line is a complete thought. No run-ons.

**Sing Test:** Read aloud — open vowels on key words (clear, fear, here, eyes, side, lo-ove). Mouth-friendly throughout.

**Chorus Repetition:** Identical chorus x2, final chorus changes only last word ("clear" → "lo-ove") for emotional payoff. One-word shift reveals the thesis: candor IS love.

**What This Avoids:**
- No 2x2 framework language
- No "ruinous empathy" jargon
- No stat-stuffing (ten months is the only number, used for weight)
- No thesis-statement definitions
- No insider references

**Self-Critique & Fixes Applied:**
1. Draft 1 had "care personally and challenge directly" — cut as framework jargon
2. Draft 1 chorus was "Close your mouth and count to six" — too instructional, replaced with "It's not mean, it's clear" which is more emotional and comes from the podcast's origin story
3. Draft 1 bridge had Kim's name — cut to keep it universal (anyone can be the narrator)
4. Verse 2 originally said "ruinous empathy" — replaced with "every gentle nothing" which shows the concept without naming it
5. Added "lo-ove" spelling in final chorus for Suno sustained note
