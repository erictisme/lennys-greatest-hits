# Make Me Care

**Album:** Career Moves
**Source:** Nancy Duarte — "Storytelling with Nancy Duarte" (Lenny's Podcast, 2023)
**The ONE Thing:** The presenter is the mentor, not the hero. The audience holds the power. Make them long for what could be.
**Killer Line:** "You're reading from the slides but no one's reading you"

---

[Intro, strings swell slowly, piano enters]

[Verse 1, intimate, close-mic]
You cleared your throat, the room went cold
Thirty faces, arms are crossed
You built the deck, rehearsed the bold
But every word just gets lost

[Pre-Chorus, building]
You think you're center stage tonight
But that was never yours to hold

[Chorus, full strings enter, 6/8 waltz feel]
Make me care
Show me what could be
Paint the world I've never seen
And I'll follow where you lead
Make me care

[Verse 2, warmer, more confident]
She took a graph, drew it by hand
Mapped the speeches, found the thread
A rise, a fall, across the land
From what is to what's ahead

[Pre-Chorus, building]
The torch won't light the whole way through
Five feet of dark is all you need

[Chorus]
Make me care
Show me what could be
Paint the world I've never seen
And I'll follow where you lead
Make me care

[Bridge, stripped to piano and cello]
You're reading from the slides
But no one's reading you
The hero's in the chairs
Not standing at the front
Come down, come close, come through

[Chorus, final — strings at full swell, one line changed]
Make me care
Show me what could be
Paint the world I've never seen
And I will follow where you lead
We care

[Outro, strings fade, piano alone]

## Critique & Revision Notes

**Draft 1 Issues Identified:**
1. "Thirty faces, arms are crossed" — 8 syl, good. But "arms are crossed" is a bit cliche. KEEP — it's vivid and singable, and the cliche works because it's universally understood body language.
2. "She took a graph, drew it by hand" — introduces Nancy without naming her, which is good. The "she" feels like a mentor figure entering the story.
3. Pre-chorus 2: "Five feet of dark is all you need" — this is strong. Comes from Nancy's torch metaphor. Works universally.
4. Bridge: "You're reading from the slides / But no one's reading you" — this is the killer line. It's a gut-punch. Keep it front and center.
5. Bridge line "Not standing at the front" — 7 syl. Clean.
6. Final chorus: changed "I'll" to "I will" for emphasis, and final line shifts from "Make me care" to "We care" — the transformation is complete.

**Syllable Audit:**
- V1: 8/8/9/7 — consistent
