# The Rituals

**Song:** The Rituals
**Album:** The Weight
**Source:** podcasts/shishir-mehrotra.md (Shishir Mehrotra, CEO & co-founder of Coda, ex-YouTube VP)
**Key:** F#m (F-sharp minor)
**BPM:** 110
**Time:** 6/8
**Genre:** Percussive world-folk groove (CityAlight congregational energy meets world-music percussion)
**Killer Line:** "Culture's not the poster on the wall — it's Monday morning, nine o'clock"

## Critique & Revision Notes

**Draft 1 issues identified and fixed:**
1. Original chorus had "golden flame" — pretty but empty, no connection to teams or rituals. Replaced with "golden thread" — thread connects, weaves, holds together. More honest.
2. "This is how we stake our claim" was thesis-statement territory. Replaced with the three-beat chant structure ("Name it — teach it — pass it on") which embodies Bing Gordon's three rules AND makes the rhythm itself the ritual.
3. "Silence finds its tongue" was cliche. Replaced with "Every voice becomes the one" — concrete, shows the Pulse ritual (everyone writes, then reveals).
4. Bridge completely rewritten. "Culture's not the poster on the wall" — the killer line. Shows don't-define by immediately following with the scene: "It's Monday morning, nine o'clock." The abstract is earned by the concrete.
5. "Not the poster — the belief" was too abstract. Moved the poster image to the bridge where it has room to breathe and lands harder.
6. Added the Gusto offer-call scene in V3 ("Strangers call you for the offer / Every hand says welcome here") — shows a specific ritual through action.
7. Final chorus changes one line: "first Friday" → "last Friday" and "it's in your bones" → "it's who you've become." The ritual has done its work — identity formed through repetition.
8. Outro mirrors the intro in reverse — percussion layers drop off one by one, ending with finger snaps alone. The ritual continues even as the song fades.

**Syllable check:**
- V1: 7/7/9/8 — consistent, 6/8 friendly
- V2: 7/8/9/9 — consistent
- Chorus: 7/7/8/9 — shorter than verses, chant-like
- V3: 7/9/9/8 — consistent
- Bridge: 10/8/9/8 — slightly longer lines for the intimate section
- Final Chorus: 7/7/8/10 — matches earlier choruses, final line stretches for payoff

**Sing test:** Read every line aloud in 6/8 time. The chorus chant ("Name it — teach it — pass it on") falls naturally into three groups of two beats. Open vowels on "on," "thread," "bones," "wall," "all," "become." The dashes create natural breath points for congregational singing.

**End-stopped check:** Every line is a complete phrase. No run-ons. Each line stands alone and can be sung independently.

**Chorus repetition:** First two choruses identical. Final chorus changes "first Friday" → "last Friday" and "it's in your bones" → "it's who you've become" — one conceptual shift for emotional landing.

**Stat check:** No numbers in lyrics. "First Friday" and "second day" are temporal markers, not stats. Clean.
