# The OKR

## Song Metadata
- **Source:** The ultimate guide to OKRs | Christina Wodtke (Stanford)
- **Album:** The Weight
- **Genre:** Indie folk-pop waltz (Stephen Day meets CityAlight)
- **Key:** Eb major
- **BPM:** 92
- **Time Signature:** 3/4
- **Sonic Signature:** Ticking clock percussion / metronome-like rim clicks on every beat
- **Vocal:** Warm male tenor, close-mic, earnest delivery
- **Mood:** Contemplative conviction — a weekly ritual that transforms everything

## The ONE Thing
The power of OKRs isn't in the measurement. It's in the weekly ritual of stopping to ask what matters.

## Killer Line
"A good goal leaves you uncomfortable but not doomed"

## Self-Critique (Rick Rubin Mode)

**What works:**
- The 3/4 waltz mirrors the quarterly rhythm naturally
- "What did I do this week?" is immediately singable and memorable
- The accountability group story in V3 is specific without being insider
- Bridge contains the killer line and the thesis without being preachy
- Final chorus shift from "I" to "we" is the only variation — earned emotional payoff

**What needed fixing (already addressed):**
- DRAFT 1 had "OKRs are a vitamin, not a medicine" as a direct quote in the chorus — too on-the-nose, moved to verse as paraphrase
- DRAFT 1 bridge was 6 lines and too dense — cut to 4 couplets with breathing room
- V3 originally named Christina by name — removed for universality
- Checked all lines: 6-11 syllables each, end-stopped, open vowels on key words (seek, week, celebrate, room, start)

**Remaining risks:**
- "Column-wide" in V1 is slightly technical but visual enough to work
- Bridge has 4 lines that are longer (9-10 syl) — acceptable for a bridge section that strips back instrumentation
- "Cadence is the product / not the chart" could read as insider — but "cadence" and "chart" are everyday words, and the meaning is clear in context

**Verdict:** Ships. The waltz feel and metronome click give this a unique sonic identity. The song is about a practice, and it FEELS like a practice.
