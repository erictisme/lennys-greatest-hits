**Album:** The Craft
**Source:** podcasts/dylan-field.md (Dylan Field, CEO & co-founder of Figma)

**Genre:** Jazz-pop groove (Tom Misch jazz guitar meets Jacob Collier harmonic sophistication)

## Critique & Revision Notes

**Draft 1 issues identified and fixed:**
1. Original chorus ("A hypothesis you dress") was too clever/analytical for jazz-pop groove. Replaced with "Call it intuition, call it taste" — conversational, singable, open vowels.
2. "Fanboy-confessed" was a forced compound. Replaced with "called it research" — drier humor, more natural delivery.
3. "One plus one is one and a half" was a direct quote lecture. Softened to "One and one is sometimes one and a half" — flows better, still gets the concept.
4. Bridge was fragmented. Rewrote around "I've been tracing your lines" — lifts Dylan's actual fanboy moment into a universal image of admiration and aspiration.
5. Killer line: "I've been tracing your lines since before we ever met" — works on two levels (literal icon tracing + following someone's work). Quotable, emotional, concrete.
6. Final chorus changes two words: "never say" → "never prove" and "won't erase" → "and it's enough." Two-word shifts for emotional payoff on the landing.

**Syllable check:**
- V1: 9/10/10/10 — consistent
- V2: 9/9/10/10 — consistent
- Chorus: 9/9/9/7 — shorter than verses, hooks breathe
- V3: 10/10/9/9 — consistent
- Bridge: 6/8/8/7 — stripped, intimate feel
- Final Chorus: 9/9/9/7 — matches earlier choruses

**Sing test:** Read every line aloud. Open vowels on "taste," "say," "stays," "erase," "lines," "met," "prove," "enough." Lines breathe naturally. Jazz-pop conversational delivery works throughout.

**End-stopped check:** Every line is a complete phrase. No run-ons. Each line stands alone.

**Chorus repetition:** First two choruses identical. Final chorus changes "never say" → "never prove" and "won't erase" → "and it's enough" — two small shifts for emotional landing.

**Stat check:** V2 has "three and a half years" (one number). V3 has "one and one" / "one and a half" (conceptual, not stat-stuffing). Under limit.
