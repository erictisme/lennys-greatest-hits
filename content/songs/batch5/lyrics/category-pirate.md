## CRITIQUE (Rick Rubin Mode)

**What's working:**
- The pirate/sailing metaphor is consistent and felt, not explained
- "Plant the flag where no one's standing" is a strong killer line
- The Elisha Otis "vertical railway" reference in V2 is specific and earned
- The bridge uses the 76% stat without stat-stuffing — "seventy-six cents of every dollar" is conversational
- Energy builds naturally through structure
- The mouse trap opening is immediately recognizable

**What needs fixing:**
1. **"She called it vertical railway"** — Elisha Otis was a man. Change the pronoun.
2. **"Make the waters mine"** — the jump from "you" perspective (pre-chorus) to "mine" (chorus) is jarring. The song should be in second person ("you") throughout until the final chorus shift.
3. **Chorus line "Don't fight for the throne they're guarding"** — 10 syllables, but rhythmically dense. Could be tighter.
4. **Bridge line "Seventy-six cents of every dollar"** — 10 syllables, fine technically, but "seventy-six" is a lot of consonants to stomp-sing. Consider restructuring.
5. **V2 "Building brand new things"** — vague filler. Make it more concrete.
6. **"Feature list a mile long"** — close to jargon. Keep it but it's borderline.
7. **Final chorus change "mine" → "ours"** is good but needs setup. The song hasn't established a "we" yet.
8. **"Waited by the phone"** — cliche. Can we make this more vivid?

## REWRITE

Fixing all issues:
1. Changed "She" to "He" for Otis
2. Made chorus consistent in second person with "yours" not "mine"
3. Tightened "Don't fight for the throne they're guarding" → "Don't storm the gates they're guarding"
4. Made bridge stat line more singable
5. Made V2 L4 more concrete
6. Replaced "Waited by the phone" with something more visual
7. Final chorus earns "ours" by bridge having set up the shared lesson
8. Small tweaks throughout for singability
