## Chord Progressions

**Verse:** Gb - Dbm7 - Ebm7 - Abm7 (i.e., I - vm7 - vi7 - iim7 — warm jazz movement)
**Chorus:** Ebm7 - Abm7 - Db - Gb (vi - ii - V - I — resolving, flowing)
**Bridge:** Bmaj7 - Ebm7 - Abm7 - Db (IV - vi - ii - V — suspended tension before release)

**Why these chords:** The jazz voicings (m7, maj7) create warmth without being predictable. The verse moves in gentle steps like water finding its path. The chorus resolves back to Gb with a sense of arrival — the marketplace finding its flow. The bridge lifts to the IV (Bmaj7) for that moment of realization before dropping back.

## Critique & Rewrite Notes

**First Draft Issues Fixed:**
1. Original V1 had "We were the underdog, employee thirty" — too on-the-nose, restructured to show the scene
2. Original chorus had "Liquidity's the pulse" — thesis statement, replaced with river metaphor
3. Original V2 tried to fit the GM rental story AND the mentor story — too much, cut to just mentors for focus
4. Bridge originally explained marketplace dynamics — replaced with the "thrill of the sale" scene from the Thumbtack story (pros who hated the booking system because they loved the chase)
5. Pre-chorus originally said "You can't engineer the human heart" — too abstract, changed to "We read the numbers from our tower high / But numbers never told us why" (references Ben's "ivory tower" quote)
6. Counted every syllable line by line, trimmed "And half the city couldn't even drive" from 11 to keep it in range
