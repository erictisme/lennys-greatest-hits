## Critique & Revision Notes

**Draft 1 issues identified:**
1. "Four kids chasing something close enough" — could be vague. But it grounds the mechanical engineering pivot without jargon. Keeping it.
2. "Threw away the old and learned to stay" — shows the pivot to coding tools. Works.
3. "Exponential starts out feeling small" — direct from Michael's quote. Strong.
4. Verse 3 has two numbers (sixty, three hundred million) — right at the limit. Acceptable because they're in separate lines and both land.
5. "Three hundred milliseconds to decide" — specific, concrete, singable. Keeping it.
6. Bridge — "Taste will matter more than syntax" is the thesis boiled to one image. Not a definition, a declaration.
7. Final chorus: only change is "we mean" → "we dream" on line 6. One-word shift for emotional payoff.

**Syllable check:**
- V1: 9/9/9/9 — consistent
- V2: 9/9/9/11 — acceptable (±2)
- Chorus: 5/8/7/7/5/8/8/9 — shorter than verses, emotionally open
- V3: 10/9/9/10 — consistent
- Bridge: 8/10/8/7 — fine

**Sing test:** Read every line aloud. Open vowels on "code," "soul," "mean," "dream," "seen," "light," "bright." Lines breathe. End-stopped throughout.

**End-stopped check:** Every line is a complete phrase. No run-ons.

**Chorus repetition:** Identical across first two instances. Final chorus changes only "we mean" → "we dream."
