# Batch 5 Feedback (Rules from Batches 1-4)

## What Went Well in Previous Batches
- Research phase extracts rich, specific material from Lenny MCP
- Suno prompts with unique sonic signatures produce distinctive tracks
- Rick Rubin producer mindset prevents generic output
- Chorus repetition rule makes songs singable

## Rules for Batch 5 (MANDATORY)

### From Batch 1
- NO thesis-statement choruses ("Product-market fit means...")
- NO stat-stuffing — one or two numbers per verse MAX
- NO insider jargon that alienates non-tech listeners
- NO filler rhymes that exist only to rhyme

### From Batch 2
- Genre must match content's emotional energy — NOT default to rock
- Consult artist-palette.md for Eric's actual taste: acoustic indie/neo-soul/folk/lo-fi
- Include the critique/rewrite step — do NOT skip it
- Every line must pass the sing test (read aloud)

### From Batch 3
- Chorus repeats IDENTICALLY — only final chorus may vary 1 line for emotional payoff
- Every song needs a UNIQUE sonic signature (specific instrument or texture that defines it)
- Diversify keys: use F#, Bb, Ab, Db, Gb, B — not just G/C/D
- Diversify time signatures: include 6/8, 3/4, 12/8 — not just 4/4
- BPM spread: 72-120 range, don't cluster around 85-95
- Mandatory variety: no two consecutive songs in the same genre
- Each song needs a ticking clock/muted trumpet/finger snaps/etc — a sonic identity

### From Batch 4
- Iconic episodes need epic treatment — these are foundational
- Push toward anthemic where appropriate (strategy, category design) but keep acoustic intimacy for personal topics (resilience, listening, ikigai)
- Emotional stakes are higher: these topics define careers and companies

### Batch 5 Specific
- This is the FINAL BATCH before contest deadline. 20 songs. Quality over speed — every song must earn its spot.
- With 87 total songs after this batch, the album catalog needs to feel COMPLETE. These 20 fill the last remaining iconic gaps.
- DIVERSITY IS CRITICAL with 20 songs: plan genre/key/BPM spread across ALL 20 before starting. Don't end up with 10 acoustic folk songs.
- Consider the album balance: Growth Engine, The Craft, The Weight, The Startup Life, Career Moves, The Human Side need equal representation.
- Strategy songs (Roger Martin, Rumelt, Lochhead) should feel DIFFERENT from each other despite similar topics — vary energy, perspective, genre.
- Personal/human songs (Dr. Becky, Rachel Lockett, Tom Conrad/Ikigai) should be the most emotionally honest — strip everything back.
- Design/craft songs (Katie Dill, Dylan Field, Bob Baxley) need to FEEL beautiful — match the message to the sonic experience.
