TITLE: Beautiful Things

STYLE: Piano-driven elegant pop in F# major, 86 BPM, 4/4 time. Stephen Day warmth meets Jacob Collier harmonic sophistication. Grand piano as the foundation — warm, resonant, slightly reverbed — with a featured violin melody that weaves through the song like a silver thread, entering in the chorus and soaring in the final chorus. NO guitar. Breathy female vocal, close-mic, intimate and tender — think Phoebe Bridgers' restraint with Norah Jones' warmth. Subtle programmed drums enter in verse 2 — brushed snare, soft kick on 1 and 3, light hi-hat. Strings pad underneath choruses, swelling gently. Bridge strips to piano only — naked, vulnerable. Final chorus blooms with full string arrangement and violin taking a melodic counter-line above the vocal. Mood: quiet pride, the tenderness of someone who cares deeply about details in a world that tells them not to bother.

SOURCE: Building beautiful products with Stripe's Head of Design | Katie Dill (Stripe, Airbnb, Lyft)
