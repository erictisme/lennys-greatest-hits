# Research Brief: The Rise of Cursor

**Source:** podcasts/michael-truell.md
**Guest:** Michael Truell (co-founder and CEO, Anysphere / Cursor)
**Album:** Electric Dreams
**Date:** 2025-05-01

---

## Story Arc

Four MIT grads passionate about AI spend months building tools for mechanical engineers — a field they don't love. They come to their senses, pivot to the white-hot center of AI coding, and build a prototype editor from scratch in five weeks. Within three months they ship it. Within 20 months they hit $100M ARR. Within two years, $300M ARR. The secret: dogfooding relentlessly, building custom models nobody expected them to build, and staying paranoid while riding a consistent exponential. Michael's vision: a world "after code" where engineers become logic designers, specifying intent rather than syntax. Taste replaces carefulness. The future arrives gradually, not all at once.

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- $0 to $100M ARR in 20 months (historic pace)
- $0 to $300M ARR in 2 years
- First prototype built from scratch in ~5 weeks, team was living on it full-time
- Shipped to public within 3 months of first line of code
- ~60 people total (high % engineering/research/design)
- Every magic moment in Cursor involves a custom model
- Autocomplete needs response in under 300 milliseconds
- Two-day in-person work test for every hire
- Started end of 2021 / beginning of 2022
- Code Pilot (GitHub Copilot beta) was the first AI product they found "really, really useful"

## Emotional Core

**Wonder at the approaching future.** This isn't a hustler story or a growth-hack story. It's a story about people who saw the future clearly — a world where the gap between intention and creation collapses — and then quietly, stubbornly built toward it. The emotion is forward momentum mixed with awe: the feeling of standing at the edge of something enormous that will take decades to fully arrive. There's also a thread of humility — "we're very, very far away from that end goal" — even while riding historic growth.

## The ONE Thing

**The distance between what you imagine and what you can build is about to collapse.** Programming is evolving from translating intent into syntax toward pure intent specification. The future isn't chatbots replacing engineers — it's engineers becoming logic designers, with taste mattering more than syntax.

## Emotional Direction

**Forward-rushing wonder with a soft landing.** Think standing on a moving walkway that keeps accelerating — not scary, but exhilarating. The verses tell the story (mechanical engineering pivot, building from scratch, the exponential that feels slow). The chorus captures the vision (a world after code, intent becoming creation). The bridge should be the quiet moment of humility: "We're very far from where we're going / But the direction's never been this clear."

**Sonic feel:** The 1975's dreamy synth-pop side meets Doris Club warmth. Glitchy processed textures that feel like code compiling. Forward motion. Not aggressive — wonder, not conquest. Ab major for warmth with electronic shimmer.
