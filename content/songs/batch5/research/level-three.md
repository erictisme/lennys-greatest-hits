# Research Brief — Level Three

## Source
- **Title:** A guide to difficult conversations, building high-trust teams, and designing a life you love
- **Guest:** Rachel Lockett (executive coach, former HR leader at Pinterest & Stripe)
- **Date:** 2025-11-23
- **Type:** Podcast

## Story Arc
Rachel Lockett, a former UX researcher turned executive coach, reveals that the biggest gap in technical leadership isn't strategy or skill — it's the inability to truly listen. She teaches three levels of listening: Level 1 (internal/distracted), Level 2 (focused/repeating words), and Level 3 (global — hearing what's beneath the words, reading body language, tone, context). She demonstrates live on Lenny, asking him about fatherhood, then reflecting back not just his words but the squirming in his chair, the eye contact avoidance, the tension between joy and exhaustion. Lenny says "it feels really nice to be heard" — and that becomes the thesis. The episode also covers the GROW coaching model, NVC (Nonviolent Communication), burnout prevention through living in your gifts, and the loneliness epidemic in tech. Rachel's personal story: she was a PM at Remind, struggling with product decisions at 2 AM, until a colleague told her "your gift is not being a product strategist — you're a people person." She was offended, then sat with it, and it changed her career.

## Emotional Core
The feeling of being truly heard — not just your words, but what's underneath them. The vulnerability required to seek help, to admit you don't have all the answers, to let someone see the squirm in your chair. And the profound relief when someone does see you — "it feels really nice to be heard." This is about the intimacy of listening, the courage it takes to stop solving and start attuning, and the loneliness that builds when nobody's operating at Level Three.

## The ONE Thing
**Level Three listening — hearing what someone is communicating, not just what they're saying — is the rarest and most transformative leadership skill.** It's the difference between solving someone's problem and seeing their soul. Most people go through their whole career at Level One (distracted, waiting for their turn to talk). Dropping into Level Three changes everything — your team, your co-founder, your marriage, your life.

## Emotional Direction
This should feel like a late-night conversation where someone finally stops talking and starts listening. Intimate, almost uncomfortably close. The sonic space should mirror Level Three listening itself — quiet enough to hear breath, pauses, the room. Think Bon Iver's "Skinny Love" meets Jonathan Ogden's whispered worship. The song should SOUND like what it's about: the act of truly hearing someone.

**Key tension:** The loneliness of never being heard vs. the warmth of someone finally seeing you.
**Perspective:** Second person — "you" talking to someone, or first person realizing they've never really listened.
