# Research Brief: PLG (Product-Led Growth)

## Source
- **Episode:** Elena Verna on how B2B growth is changing, product-led growth, product-led sales, why you should go freemium not trial, what features to make free, and much more
- **Guest:** Elena Verna (growth leader at Miro, SurveyMonkey, Amplitude)
- **Date:** 2022-06-23
- **File:** podcasts/elena-verna-30.md

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Elena opens with growth tactics that NEVER work — hiring a growth team to find product-market fit for you, rebranding/redesigning your marketing site, and chasing growth hacks from social media. These are expensive distractions. Most startups are sales-led by default and burning cash to acquire every single user.

**Turning Point:** The shift to product-led growth. Instead of paying for every customer through ad spend and sales calls, you let the product itself become the acquisition engine. The key insight: freemium beats free trial EVERY time. Free trial puts a clock on value. Freemium lets users experience real value with no pressure, and they become your marketing channel — inviting teammates, sharing boards, spreading the product organically.

**End:** But PLG doesn't mean "fire your sales team." The real unlock is product-led SALES — the product qualifies leads (who's active? who hit the usage ceiling?) and sales closes the warm ones. Elena's Miro community example: she was banging her head against the wall trying to build it solo, then talked to someone at Atlassian who asked "user community or partner community?" — her mind was blown. The meta-lesson: don't reinvent what others have already solved.

## Specific Numbers & Facts
- Elena led growth at Miro (CMO), SurveyMonkey (SVP Growth), Amplitude (Head of Growth)
- Miro went from internal tool to 50M+ users through PLG
- Freemium converts at lower rates but generates more total revenue than free trials
- "Never once" has a rebrand produced good performance results
- Free users become the marketing channel — each one invites others

## Emotional Core
The thrill and relief of building something so good it spreads on its own. The old way (sales-led) is exhausting — paying for every user, cold-calling, grinding. The new way (product-led) is watching your product do the work while you sleep. It's the difference between pushing a boulder uphill and building something with its own gravity.

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches
**Let the product be the salesperson.** Give real value away free, and users will bring you more users. The product sells itself while you sleep.
