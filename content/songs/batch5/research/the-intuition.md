**Date:** 2024-06-30

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Dylan Field was a kid actor in eToys commercials at age five. His differentiators were that he could read, sit still, and was "decently cute." He hit puberty and those stopped being differentiators — so he pivoted to computer science. Interned at LinkedIn, saw network visualization tools, got fascinated by the design community's social graph.

**Turning Point:** Built a Twitter scraper to map the most influential designers, reached out as a fanboy to buy them coffee. Traced Tim Van Damme's icons from Dribbble because they were beautiful — used them as test cases for vector networks. It took 3.5 years to launch Figma, ~5 years to get the first paying customer. Sho joined as director of engineering and in week one gave a presentation: "Here's the gap. Let's go." That catalyzed shipping.

**End:** Figma became the design tool shaping the world's technology. Dylan sees it as a responsibility, not a trophy. He's still reading every tweet about Figma, still furrowing his brow when things aren't simple enough. The community of thousands at Config exists because a college dropout traced icons and bought strangers coffee.

## Emotional Core

A college kid with no design credentials but an obsessive curiosity — tracing strangers' icons, scraping Twitter graphs, buying coffee for his heroes — built the tool that now shapes the world's digital interfaces. The lesson isn't about coding or design skill. It's about trusting your taste, keeping things simple when everything pushes toward complexity, and treating your intuition as a starting point for hypothesis, not an answer.

---

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches

**Trust your intuition — not as an answer, but as the question worth asking.** Your gut feeling is a hypothesis generator. Test it, refine it, follow it. The people who build beautiful things aren't always the ones with credentials — they're the ones who know what they like, chase it relentlessly, and keep stripping away until only the essential remains.
