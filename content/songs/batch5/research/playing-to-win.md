## Story Arc

Roger Martin — world's #1 management thinker, former Rotman School dean, strategy advisor for 42 years — walks Lenny through his "strategy choice cascade": five questions every person (not just CEOs) must answer to build a real strategy. The emotional core: strategy is hard because it's **intellectually challenging AND emotionally intimidating**. You have to make choices that cut things off. Most people avoid that pain and end up "playing to play" instead of playing to win. But great strategists aren't born — they practice. The episode culminates in Martin's "betterment" philosophy: don't try to solve everything, just close the most painful gap, then the next one, then the next.

## Emotional Core

The intimidation of choice. Strategy asks you to pick a lane and kill the others. Most people flinch. They hedge, they play to play, they avoid the cut. Roger Martin says the real courage isn't brilliance — it's the willingness to say "we will do THIS and not THAT" when the stakes are high and you could be wrong. The liberating counterpoint: you don't have to be a genius. You just have to practice closing one gap at a time. Water finds its level.

## The ONE Thing

Strategy is making choices that scare you — cutting off options to protect the ones that matter. Playing to win means accepting the emotional weight of deciding what you will NOT do.
