# Research Brief: Beautiful Things

## Source
- **Episode:** Building beautiful products with Stripe's Head of Design | Katie Dill (Stripe, Airbnb, Lyft)
- **Guest:** Katie Dill (Head of Design at Stripe, former Head of Design at Lyft, Head of Experience Design at Airbnb)
- **Date:** 2023-10-15
- **File:** podcasts/katie-dill.md

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Katie arrives at Airbnb, hired to lead the 10-person design team. She comes in swinging — ready to fix everything she learned was broken during her interviews. One month in, five designers and an HR partner sit her down at a gray table in a white room, reading from papers all the things she's doing wrong. It's an intervention. The missing piece: she hadn't earned their trust. She was inflicting change instead of building change together.

**Turning Point:** Katie listens. Doesn't defend. Takes it all in. She shifts from "come in swinging" to "come in listening." Within months, the design team goes from the lowest engagement scores to the best in the company. The lesson crystallizes into a philosophy she carries to Lyft and then Stripe: beauty is not the opposite of function — beauty IS function. The word "beauty" has been declining in books since the 1800s, as if caring about how things look and feel is frivolous. But beautiful things increase trust. When users see painstaking detail, they trust you care about the details they can't see.

**End:** At Stripe, Katie operationalizes beauty through "walking the store" — leaders review 15 essential user journeys quarterly, friction-logging every detail the way a store owner walks their floor. She introduces the formula: Performance = Potential minus Interference. Her life motto, written on a Post-It in her jewelry box: "Tomorrow is today." The gravitational pull is always toward mediocrity. You fight it every day, like going to the gym — one workout won't give you abs, but skipping one makes it easy to skip the next. Quality is growth. Stripe saw 10.5% revenue increase from checkout quality improvements alone.

## Emotional Core
The tender courage it takes to care about beauty in a world that tells you function is all that matters. Katie's story is about fighting the gravitational pull toward mediocrity — not with force, but with listening, trust, and relentless attention to detail. The emotional core is the quiet pride of someone who sweats the details because they know those details are what make people feel something.

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches
**Beauty is not decoration — it's how you earn trust.** When people see you care about the details they CAN see, they trust you with the details they can't.
