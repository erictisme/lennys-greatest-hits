# Research Brief: Resilience Over Happiness

## Story Arc

Dr. Becky Kennedy — clinical psychologist, author, and CEO of "Good Inside" — built a parenting empire on a radical insight: the same principles that help parents raise resilient kids also make better leaders. The conversation opens with Shreyas Doshi's observation that "most adults in the corporate environment are really just babies in disguise." Becky reframes: all humans need the same things at every age. When needs aren't met, we act out — whether we're two or forty-five.

**Beginning:** Repair is the #1 relationship strategy. Secure attachment isn't defined by getting it right — it's defined by being willing to go back and take responsibility when you didn't. "Perfect is creepy."

**Turning Point:** The "resilience over happiness" chapter — Becky's core philosophy. She pushes back on "Don't you just want your kid to be happy?" with conviction: "Optimizing for happiness in childhood is the quickest way to build anxiety and fragility in adulthood. Hard stop." Children (and employees) need to experience discomfort, difficulty, and disappointment — and be supported through it, not shielded from it.

**End:** The mantra lands: "This is hard and I can do hard things." Not "this is hard, so someone must be at fault and it should be easier." The job of a sturdy leader — parent or manager — is to hold both truths simultaneously: "I believe you" (your struggle is real) AND "I believe in you" (you're capable of more than you see right now).

## Specific Numbers & Facts

## Emotional Core
**The courage to let someone struggle.** Every instinct says protect, fix, smooth it over. But the deepest love is standing beside someone in their difficulty without rescuing them from it. A child screaming over a puzzle. An employee drowning in a hard project. The sturdy leader doesn't solve it for them — they say "I see you, this is real, and I know you can do this." It's the hardest form of love because it looks like inaction when it's actually the bravest thing you can do.

## The ONE Thing
**The people you love most will grow strongest not from your protection, but from your presence during their struggle.**
