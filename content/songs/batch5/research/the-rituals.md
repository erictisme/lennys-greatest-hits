## Story Arc

Shishir Mehrotra — co-founder/CEO of Coda, former VP of product and engineering at YouTube, six years at Microsoft, board member of Spotify — is writing a book called "Rituals of Great Teams." The origin: a Reid Hoffman podcast interview where Shishir shared a quote from Bing Gordon (co-founder of EA): "Great companies have a very small list of golden rituals." Bing's three rules: (1) they're named, (2) every employee knows them by their first Friday, and (3) they're templated. The concept exploded — Shishir started a dinner series, every third Wednesday, where leaders shared rituals. He's now interviewed over 1,000 people. The core insight: rituals are a "mirror of culture." Dharmesh Shah (HubSpot) told him: "We build two products — one for customers, one for employees. The employee product is called culture." And the way people describe their culture? Through rituals.

## Emotional Core
**The power of shared rhythm.** Great teams aren't great by accident — they have recurring patterns that become identity. A Monday standup, a Friday demo, a named way of making decisions. These rituals feel small from the outside but they ARE the culture from the inside. The new hire who learns Dory/Pulse on day one isn't just learning a meeting format — they're learning "we listen to everyone here, rank doesn't win." Rituals make the invisible visible. They turn values on a wall into behaviors you can see, repeat, and pass on.

## The ONE Thing
**Culture isn't what you say — it's what you repeat.** The rituals a team keeps, names, and templates become the actual lived culture. Not mission statements. Not values decks. The things you do every Monday, every Friday, every time someone new walks in.
