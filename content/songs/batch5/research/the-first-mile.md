# Research Brief: The First Mile

## Source
- **Episode:** Lessons on building product sense, navigating AI, optimizing the first mile, and making it through the messy middle | Scott Belsky (Adobe, Behance)
- **Guest:** Scott Belsky
- **Date:** 2023-05-18
- **Source File:** podcasts/scott-belsky.md

## Story Arc

Scott Belsky — founder of Behance, CPO of Adobe — has spent his career obsessed with the moment a user first touches a product. His core insight: in the first 30 seconds, every user is lazy, vain, and selfish. Not as an insult — as psychology. Your product has to meet people in their most primal state. Most teams spend the "final mile" of their build time on the "first mile" of the customer experience — and that mismatch is where products die.

The broader arc: conviction drives everything. In the "messy middle" (his book's title), founders must ask themselves — knowing what I know now, do I have MORE or LESS conviction? If more, stick with it. If less, quit. Life is short.

He also introduces the "golden gut" — the experienced product thinker's instinct to reduce cognitive load, strip steps, and use "presumptuous defaults" instead of asking users to configure everything. The golden gut says: what if I just remove this altogether?

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- **30 seconds** — the window where users are lazy, vain, and selfish
- **Behance** — Belsky's company, sold to Adobe
- **Photoshop** — used to cost hundreds of dollars, now $10/month — the funnel widened, so onboarding had to change completely
- **10% confused vs 90% faster** — the golden gut trade-off: accept that 10% may get confused if 90% move faster
- **3 steps on one page vs 1 step across 3 pages** — the micro-design decisions that define product craft

## Emotional Core

The tension between how much love and effort you pour into building something vs. the brutal reality that a user gives you 30 seconds of their most distracted, selfish attention. It's humbling and clarifying. The emotion isn't despair — it's radical acceptance. Once you accept people are lazy, vain, and selfish in those first seconds, you can actually design for them. It's a form of empathy through honesty.

## The ONE Thing

**Your product gets 30 seconds. The user is lazy, vain, and selfish. Design for THAT person — not the person you wish they were.**

## 3 Chorus Hook Ideas

## Emotional Direction

**Playful provocation.** This isn't a sad song. It's a wry, knowing smile. The energy is: "Yeah, I'm lazy, vain, and selfish — and you STILL have to win me over."
