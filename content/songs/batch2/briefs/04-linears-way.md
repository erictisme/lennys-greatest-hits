## The ONE Thing
Trust your taste over your metrics — the best products come from people who care enough to say "this doesn't feel right yet" instead of "the numbers say ship it."

## Chord Progressions

### Verse: Em - C - G - D
The classic indie rock engine. Em opens with tension, C lifts to hope, G gives space, D drives forward. This keeps the verse moving without resolving — you're always leaning into the next line, which mirrors the dissatisfaction Karri feels with the status quo.

### Chorus: C - G - D - Em
Inverted from the verse — starts on the IV (C) for an immediate lift that signals "this is the hook." The G-D creates momentum, and landing on Em gives the chorus a defiant minor resolution. The "doesn't feel right yet" line landing on Em is perfect — the minor chord IS the "not right yet" feeling.

### Bridge: Am - Em - C - D
Darker and more introspective. Am-Em is moody and uncertain (the fear of betting on taste), then C-D opens up and pushes toward the final chorus resolution. The D at the end acts as a dominant that launches you into the final chorus with cathartic energy.
