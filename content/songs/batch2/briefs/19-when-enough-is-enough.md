# When Enough Is Enough
Album: Career Moves
Genre: Cinematic piano ballad
Mood: Raw, breaking, cathartic
BPM: 72
Key: Cm (resolves to C major in final chorus)
Reference: Lewis Capaldi raw vulnerability, Coldplay "Fix You" quiet-to-anthemic build, Bon Iver intimacy and space
Source: When enough is enough | Andy Johns (ex-FB, Twitter, Quora) — Lenny's Podcast
Source Date: 2023-09-10
Topics: burnout, mental health, identity, walking away, self-worth
JTBD Category: Stressed and overwhelmed / Career development
Chord Progression: Cm - Ab - Eb - Bb | Ab - Eb - Bb - Cm | Fm - Ab - Bb - G

## The ONE Thing
Your body's alarm — broken sleep, broken heart, broken teeth — is telling you something your ambition won't let you hear: it's time to stop climbing and start living.

## Killer Line
"Of a building I was dying for" — double meaning: dying for (desperately wanting) and literally dying inside. The entire emotional tension of the song in one line.

## Chord Progressions

### Verse: Cm - Ab - Eb - Bb (i - VI - III - VII)
Arpeggiated piano, slow and breathing. Dark emotional weight with Ab to Eb creating a brief lift before Bb pulls back into tension. Matches the "still climbing even though I'm breaking" energy.

### Chorus: Ab - Eb - Bb - Cm (VI - III - VII - i)
Opens on Ab for a more expansive, raw feel. Landing on Cm at the end grounds the devastation. The openness makes "Enough, enough" feel like a plea, not a statement.

### Bridge: Fm - Ab - Bb - G (iv - VI - VII - V)
Fm adds depth and darkness. The G major chord is the turning point — bright, unexpected major in a minor key. This is the moment Andy finds the boy inside himself. The harmonic surprise mirrors the emotional breakthrough.

### Final Chorus: Ab - Eb - Bb/D - C (VI - III - VII - I)
Resolves to C MAJOR instead of C minor. The shift from minor to major on the final chorus represents the transformation — from despair to acceptance. Floating down the river instead of climbing the mountain.

### Outro: C - F - C (fading)
Simple, spacious, in C major. Piano only, no drums. The river carrying him. Let the silence do the work.
