## Chord Progressions

### Verse: Bb - Gm - Eb - F (I - vi - IV - V)
Classic pop progression — driving and forward-moving. The story is building momentum through two failures toward the breakthrough. The steady progression mirrors Jorge's persistence.

### Chorus: Eb - F - Gm - Bb (IV - V - vi - I)
Starts on the IV chord for that "lifted" feeling when the hook hits. The ear feels elevated from the verse. Resolves home to Bb on "Don't break the streak" — the anchor lands with certainty.

### Bridge: Gm - Eb - F - Bb (vi - IV - V - I)
Starts on the minor vi for the reflective "it started as a number" moment. Then resolves upward through IV-V-I as the killer line lands — the music literally resolves as the insight clicks. The harmonic journey mirrors the emotional one.

**Why Bb major at 128 BPM:** Bb is bright and energetic without being shrill — perfect for synth-pop. 128 BPM is dance-adjacent, creating an almost involuntary physical response (head-nodding, foot-tapping) that mirrors the compulsive "one more day" feeling of a streak.

## Killer Line
> Each day you come back, you care a little more — that's the math they never taught before

Paraphrases Jorge Mazal's insight: "Each day that a learner comes to Duolingo, they care a bit more about coming back the next day than they did the day before." Transforms a retention metric into a universal truth about human commitment.
