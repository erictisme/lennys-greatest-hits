## The ONE Thing
Every great marketplace started by doing tiny, unscalable, unglamorous things — knocking on doors, delivering food yourself — until the flywheel caught and spun on its own.

## Killer Line
"Nobody's coming till somebody's here" — the marketplace paradox in one phrase. Works as life advice beyond startups.

## Runner-Up Lines
- "The rider grabbed the wheel, became the ride" — the viral loop in one image
- "I let go... and it didn't fall" — the flywheel moment
- "Now they're all knocking back for more" — the motif flips: you were knocking, now they are

## Chord Progressions

**Verse: E - B/D# - C#m - A**
Classic I-V-vi-IV with a passing bass note (B/D#) for forward momentum. Bright and driving — matches the "packing bags, catching flights" energy. The descending bass line (E → D# → C# → A) mirrors falling into the problem.

**Chorus: A - E - B - E**
Starts on IV (A) for lift above the verse. Simple and anthemic — the message is clear, the chords stay out of the way. Resolves to E twice for a satisfying, fist-pumping landing on "came back for more."

**Bridge: C#m - A - B - G#m**
Starting on vi (C#m) creates a reflective, wondering quality — "how did this happen?" The unexpected G#m (iii) adds beautiful tension before crashing back into the triumphant final chorus. Represents the surprise of the flywheel catching.

### Motif Tracking
The **door/knocking** motif evolves through the song:
- V1: "Rang the doorbell"
- C1: "I went first through the door" / "one knock"
- C2: "we showed up at the door"
- Final C: "someone went first through the door" / "now they're all knocking back for more"

The flip from "I'm knocking on doors" to "they're knocking back" IS the flywheel — shown, not explained.
