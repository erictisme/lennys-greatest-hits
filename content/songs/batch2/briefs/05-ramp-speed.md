## Chord Progressions

**Verse: E - C#m - A - B**
The I-vi-IV-V drives forward relentlessly — each chord pulls to the next. The C#m adds enough tension to keep it from sounding generic, while the A-B resolution keeps the momentum building. Matches the "small team, big ambition" energy.

**Chorus: A - B - E - C#m**
Starting on the IV (A) gives the chorus an immediate lift — you feel it shift up from the verse. The V-I (B-E) is a triumphant resolution on "doing beats the diagram." Ending on C#m keeps tension alive for the next section instead of fully resolving.

**Bridge: C#m - A - E - B**
Starting on the vi (C#m) shifts the mood — suddenly warmer, more reflective. This mirrors the lyrical shift from speed to soul (Fortnite, gratitude, fixing each other's bugs). The progression resolves gently back to I-V, setting up the final chorus to hit with full force.

**Why E major at 145 BPM:** E major is one of the brightest, most energetic keys — natural home for driving guitars and synths. 145 BPM is fast enough to feel urgent without becoming frantic. This is The Killers' "Mr. Brightside" territory — a song that makes you want to move.
