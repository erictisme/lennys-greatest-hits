# Good Enough
Album: Frameworks
Genre: Indie pop / driving alternative
Mood: Urgent, liberating, cathartic
BPM: 112
Key: G major
Reference: The 1975 (rhythmic guitar pulse), Hozier (raw emotional male vocal), Bon Iver (atmospheric builds)
Source: "4 questions Shreyas Doshi wishes he'd asked himself sooner" (podcast, Shreyas Doshi)
Source Date: 2022
Topics: prioritization, perfectionism, leverage, productivity, frameworks
JTBD Category: Productivity & Prioritization
Chord Progression: Em-C-G-D | G-D-Em-C | Am-Em-C-G/B

## Chord Notes
- **Verse (Em-C-G-D):** Starts on the minor — tension, the feeling of drowning in work. The progression resolves to D, always reaching but never landing on G. You're close to relief but can't get there.
- **Chorus (G-D-Em-C):** Opens on the tonic major — the exhale, the liberation of "good enough." The move to Em in the middle keeps emotional depth.
- **Bridge (Am-Em-C-G/B):** Descending bass line creates a reflective, spiraling feeling. The G/B at the end is the pivot back to the chorus — the moment the frame shifts.

## Killer Line
"The list won't love me back" — personifies the to-do list as the thing you're sacrificing for that gives nothing in return. Works outside any tech context.

Runner-up: "Not every task you own / Deserves the you that can't sleep at night"

- [x] Show don't define — no thesis statements. The framework is shown through Shreyas's story, not defined.
- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — all lines 5-9 syllables, tight and consistent.
- [x] One killer line identified — "The list won't love me back"
- [x] Chorus varies on repetition — lines 2, 4, 6 change across all three choruses. Final chorus shifts "now I know" to "now I go."
- [x] No stat-stuffing — only "ten for one" as a number, and it serves the story.
