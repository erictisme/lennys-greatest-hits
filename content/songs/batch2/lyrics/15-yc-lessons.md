Genre: Indie folk anthem
Mood: Vulnerable, communal, defiant
BPM: 108
Key: G major
Reference: The Lumineers (building folk anthems), Noah Kahan (conversational vulnerability), Bon Iver (raw intimacy)

Source Date: 2023-03-02
Topics: founder struggle, YC, vulnerability, customer discovery, resilience
JTBD Category: Starting & scaling a startup
Chord Progression: G - Em - C - G | C - G - D - Em | Am - Em - C - D

## Chord Notes

**Key: G major at 108 BPM** — warm and open, the workhorse key of folk anthems. Mid-tempo walking pace that builds to a clap-along.

- **Verse (G - Em - C - G):** The G to Em drop mirrors the emotional shift from "I'm fine" to the real feeling underneath. Fingerpicked acoustic, intimate. Open chords ring out warm but melancholy.
- **Chorus (C - G - D - Em → C - G - D):** Lifts from the verse — C major opens it up, D creates forward motion, Em provides the ache. Second half drops Em for a pure D resolution on "we build anyway" — defiant, unresolved enough to keep pushing.
- **Bridge (Am - Em - C - D):** Minor start for the vulnerability of "mine is broken too." Am to Em is the confession; C to D is resolve building. The D hangs before crashing into the final chorus.
