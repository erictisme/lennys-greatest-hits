## Chord Progressions

### Verse: Em - C - G - D
Starts on the minor root — tension and unease. Chesky watching his company drift away. The walk through C - G - D gives forward motion, like a story unfolding. Classic indie rock movement that keeps the narrative driving.

### Chorus: C - G - D - Em (IV - I - V - vi)
Opens on the bright IV chord — the defiant moment. "I'm in the room" lands with confidence on the major lift. But the progression resolves back to Em — there's weight to this choice. Being in every room has a cost. The music says what the lyrics hint: conviction, not comfort.

### Bridge: Am - C - Em - B7
Starts unsettled on Am — the cost stings. The B7 dominant chord at the end creates unresolved tension, demanding resolution into the final chorus. It mirrors the bridge's emotional turn: acknowledging the price of founder mode, then refusing to let go anyway.

**Why Em at 138 BPM:** E minor is the key of defiance in rock — dark enough for the crisis, bright enough for the triumph. 138 BPM is driving without tipping into punk. It should feel like a founder who can't sleep and won't stop.

## Killer Lines

**Primary:** "They built a church around the thing I'd start / Then prayed to process till it lost its heart"
— This is the screenshot line. Works outside any context. Captures what happens when process replaces passion.

**Secondary:** "You can't hand off the thing only you feel"
— The core thesis of founder mode in one sentence. Embodied knowledge can't be delegated.

**Tertiary:** "If my name's on the building, I'll be there"
— Universal. Works for any founder, any creator, anyone who owns their work.

- [x] Show don't define — no thesis statements. The story IS the lesson. Never says "founder mode is..."
- [x] 6-12 syllables per line — range is 4-12 (4 only on anchor line "I'm in the room")
- [x] One killer line identified — "They built a church around the thing I'd start / Then prayed to process till it lost its heart"
- [x] Chorus varies on repetition — lines 2-3 change each time; final chorus rewrites lines 2, 3, and 4
- [x] No stat-stuffing — "a quarter" (25% layoffs) serves the narrative, only number in a verse

### Batch 1 Mistakes Avoided
- [x] Not preachy — first-person narrative, not a lecture
- [x] Real narrative arc — delegation → drift → COVID → realization → defiance → cost acknowledged
- [x] Emotional — loss, devastation, defiance, AND the weight on others (bridge)
- [x] Works as standalone song — "I'm in the room" is universal for anyone who cares about their craft
- [x] No insider jargon — no "founder mode" label in lyrics, no Lenny-specific refs
- [x] No filler rhymes — every line earns its place
- [x] End-stopped lines — each line is a complete phrase
- [x] Bridge captures the DUALITY — the cost to others, not just the founder's triumph
