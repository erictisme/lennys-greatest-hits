## The ONE Thing
Growth isn't one thing — it's a machine with parts that work together, and the secret is knowing which part to build at which stage.

## Killer Line

## Chord Progressions

**Verse: Em - C - G - D**
Minor root gives the verse urgency and grit — you're pushing a car by hand, it should feel like work. The C - G - D climb builds momentum gradually, like the kickstart phase gaining traction.

**Chorus: C - G - D - Em**
Starting on IV (C) gives the chorus an immediate lift — brighter, more open than the verse. The D resolving to Em on "cut it loose" grounds the hook with satisfying tension. Never fully resolves — always pulls you back for more.

**Bridge: Am - C - Em - D**
Am introduces a new color — darker, reflective. This is the gut-punch realization: you've been doing it wrong. The unresolved D at the end hangs in the air, pulling you into the final chorus with urgency.

**Why Em at 132 BPM:** E minor is the natural home for driving rock with electronic edge — dark enough to feel urgent, open enough for big guitar riffs. 132 BPM is fast enough to feel like a car accelerating but controlled enough for the vocal to breathe. Phoenix/Arctic Monkeys territory.
