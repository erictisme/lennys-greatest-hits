## Chord Progressions

**Key: Dm | BPM: 78**

**Verse: Dm - C - Bb - F (i - VII - VI - III)**
The minor root grounds the heaviness of avoidance and dread. The walk down through C to Bb creates a feeling of inevitability — you can't keep putting this off. The resolution to F (relative major) at the end of each 4-bar phrase gives a tiny lift of hope before cycling back to Dm.

**Chorus: Bb - F - Gm - Dm (VI - III - iv - i)**
Starting on Bb (the relative major's IV) lifts the chorus above the verse — it feels like stepping into clarity. The move to Gm adds warmth without brightness, and the resolution back to Dm reminds you this is still hard. The progression has gravity without despair.

**Bridge: Gm - Dm - Bb - A7 (iv - i - VI - V7)**
The A7 dominant creates genuine tension — the only truly unresolved chord in the song. It mirrors Mochary's vulnerability moment: the coach who has all the answers admitting he's still afraid. The V7 wants desperately to resolve back to Dm, which it does when the final chorus arrives. That resolution IS the emotional payoff.
