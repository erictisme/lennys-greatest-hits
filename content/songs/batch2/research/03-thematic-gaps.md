## Album 1: FOUNDERS — Missing Themes

### Gap 1: Fundraising / Raising Money
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 covers the *what* of founding (PMF, unscalable things, idea selection) but not the *how you fund it*. Fundraising is one of the most emotionally intense founder experiences.
**Best episode:** "Raising a seed round 101" (newsletter, 2024-09-17) — Terrence Rohan & Jack Altman, involved in 1,000+ seed rounds. 10-step framework from "should I raise VC at all?" to "should I make an announcement?" Includes founder quotes from Notion's Ivan Zhao ("Revenue is its own Series A") and LaunchDarkly's Edith Harbaugh. Specific numbers: $1M-$4M typical seed, 10-20% dilution.
**Song potential:** The emotional rollercoaster of pitching — rejection, FOMO, the VC treadmill. "Revenue is its own Series A" is a killer chorus hook.

### Gap 2: Pricing & Monetization
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 has nothing about how founders actually make money. Pricing is one of Lenny's most-covered and most-practical topics.
**Best episode:** "Freemium vs. trial" (newsletter, 2022-03-08) — Analyzed 50 SaaS products' pricing models. Key insight: 90% of freemium products also offer a trial. Surprising finding: Figma, Miro, and Amplitude are the only freemium products that DON'T offer a trial. Concrete framework for when to go freemium vs. trial vs. both.
**Song potential:** The agony of pricing — "How much is your thing worth?" The freemium trap, giving it away to get them hooked. Great for a catchy, relatable song.

### Gap 3: The Pivot
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 covers finding PMF and avoiding tarpit ideas, but not what happens when your idea ISN'T working and you need to change direction. Pivoting is the second most consequential founder decision.
**Best episode:** "The art of the pivot, part 2: How, why, and when to pivot" (newsletter, 2024-05-14) — Analyzed 40+ pivots. Key data: 1 in 3 B2B startups pivot before finding their big idea. Two types: ideation pivots vs. hard pivots. Five strategies for finding your next idea. Dalton Caldwell: "A good pivot is like going home — it's warmer." Also Stewart Butterfield (podcasts/stewart-butterfield.md, 2025-11-20) — pivoted TWICE from video games (Flickr, then Slack). "Creating the distance so you can make an intellectual, rational decision rather than an emotional one."
**Song potential:** Incredibly emotional — killing something you've poured your heart into. "Pivoting is all about opportunity cost." The Slack pivot story is legendary.

### Gap 4: Hiring Your First Team
**Why it's missing:** Building a company isn't just about the idea — it's about the people. First hires shape everything.
**Best episode:** "Hiring your early team" (newsletter, 2023-10-03) — Who should be employee #1? (Engineers, 2/3 of the time.) Where to find them? (Friends and former colleagues, by far.) How to convince them? When to hire sales? Quotes from founders of Vanta, Coda, Gusto. Specific data on first 10 hires across dozens of companies.
**Song potential:** "Your first hire shapes your culture forever." The desperation of recruiting when you're a nobody. The network effect of great people attracting great people.

## Album 2: PRODUCT — Missing Themes

### Gap 1: Prioritization / Saying No
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 covers what good product looks like (taste, 11-star, user research) but not the hardest PM skill: deciding what NOT to build.
**Best episode:** "Saying no" (newsletter, 2021-09-28) — Lenny's own framework for the 5 paths of saying no. From "Yes, and here's when we'll do it" to "No, because it's a bad idea." Also "Introducing DRICE: a modern prioritization framework" (newsletter, 2023-11-07) — Distillation from 35+ years at Dropbox, Facebook, Airtable, Opendoor. RICE → DRICE doubled their impact rate.
**Song potential:** "It's literally your job to say no." The PM as the bad guy. The relief when you finally create focus. Very singable — tension between wanting to please everyone and needing to cut.

### Gap 2: Metrics / North Star
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 has "Talk to Users" (qualitative) but nothing about the quantitative side — measuring what matters.
**Best episode:** "Breaking the rules of growth: Why Shopify bans KPIs" — Archie Abrams (podcast, 2024-11-07). Shopify's core product teams have NO metrics or KPIs — they're "essentially banned." Decisions made on taste and intuition instead. 100-year vision drives roadmap. Growth team optimizes for churn (counterintuitive). This is THE most contrarian take on metrics in the entire archive.
**Song potential:** "What if you stopped measuring everything?" The tyranny of dashboards. Shopify betting on taste over data. Incredibly counterintuitive and memorable.

### Gap 3: Roadmapping / What to Build Next
**Why it's missing:** Adjacent to prioritization but distinct — this is about the planning artifact itself and how teams decide direction.
**Best episode:** "Building better product roadmaps" — Janna Bastow (podcast, 2022-10-16). Inventor of the Now/Next/Later framework. Co-founded Mind the Product. Built ProdPad as a timeline roadmap first, then realized timeline roadmaps are broken — she never hit the dates. Pivoted to Now/Next/Later. Her story of failing at Gantt charts is relatable and funny.
**Song potential:** "Throw away the Gantt chart." The false precision of timeline roadmaps. Now/Next/Later as liberation. Light, fun song about planning gone wrong.

## Album 3: BUILDERS — Missing Themes

### Gap 1: Technical Debt / Speed vs. Quality
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 has "Ship It" (speed) and "Feature Factory" (process), but not the tension between moving fast and building well. This is THE engineering dilemma.
**Best episode:** "How to win in the AI era: Ship a feature every week, embrace technical debt" — Gaurav Misra, CEO of Captions (podcast, 2025-03-27). Every engineer ships a marketable product every week. "As a startup, your job is to take on technical debt because that is how you operate faster than a bigger company." They pay it all back in November/December. Created "design engineering" role at Snap. Specific: went from $0 to massive growth by shipping weekly.
**Song potential:** "Your job is to take on debt" — a builder anthem about speed. The November payback ritual. Design engineering as a superpower. Great energy for an upbeat track.

### Gap 2: Craft & Quality
**Why it's missing:** The flip side of speed. Batch 1 touches taste from a PM perspective, but not the builder's obsession with craft.
**Best episode:** "Inside Linear: Building with taste, craft, and focus" — Karri Saarinen (podcast, 2023-10-08). No AB tests. No metrics-based goals. One PM for the whole company. Teams assemble around projects, then disperse. "Craft starts with the people" — they hire for it. Quality as competitive advantage against incumbents. Also "Building product at Stripe: craft, metrics, and customer obsession" — Jeff Weinstein (podcast, 2024-07-11) — teaches craft systematically at Stripe.
**Song potential:** "No AB tests, no dashboards, just taste." The Linear way as a counter-culture movement. Building something beautiful in a world of MVP slop. Emotional and aspirational.

### Gap 3: Engineering Culture / Intensity
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 doesn't cover what makes great engineering organizations tick — the culture of building, not just the act.
**Best episode:** "Building a culture of excellence" — David Singleton, CTO of Stripe (podcast, 2023-05-04). Hiring is deeply personal at Stripe — they maintain long-term relationships with candidates. Didn't hire a PM until employee #200. Product-minded engineering culture. Structured hiring loops. Also "How Shopify builds a high-intensity culture" — Farhan Thawar (podcast, 2024-12-19) — three themes: hiring, creating intensity, choosing the hard path. Pair programming culture.
**Song potential:** "No PM until employee 200" — building without process. The Stripe hiring philosophy. Choosing the hard path as a way of life.

## Album 4: THE HUMAN SIDE — Missing Themes

### Gap 1: When to Quit / Career Courage
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 has Imposter (doubt) and Burnout (exhaustion) and IC Track (direction), but not the actual moment of deciding to leave — quitting a job, killing a startup, walking away.
**Best episode:** "This will make you a better decision-maker" — Annie Duke (podcast, 2024-05-02). Former pro poker player, author of "Thinking in Bets" and "Quit." Key insight: "If you're thinking about quitting, it's already probably past the time that you should have quit." The data is strong that by the time you quit, it's long overdue. Also "How to break out of autopilot and create the life you want" — Graham Weaver (podcast, 2025-01-16), Stanford GSB professor — "The failure would've been quitting."
**Song potential:** "If you're thinking about it, you're already late." The poker metaphor for knowing when to fold. Universal — applies to jobs, startups, relationships. Emotionally powerful.

### Gap 2: Leadership / Becoming a Leader
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1 covers the individual experience (imposter, burnout, IC vs manager) but not what it means to actually LEAD — the weight, the loneliness, the growth.
**Best episode:** "How to unlock your product leadership skills" — Ken Norton (podcast, 2022-07-24). Creative vs. Reactive leadership (from Leadership Circle). 75% of leaders operate reactively. "I loved managing a team of 5 because I got to coach. I hated managing 35 because there's no time for it." Also "10 contrarian leadership truths" — Matt MacInnis, Rippling (podcast, 2025-12-28) — "Hard work is not sufficient for an extraordinary outcome, but it is 100% necessary."
**Song potential:** "75% of leaders are on autopilot." Creative vs. reactive — the choice every leader faces. The loneliness of the decision seat. Could be a powerful, reflective ballad.

### Gap 3: Finding Meaning / Purpose at Work
**Why it's missing:** Batch 1's Human Side is about surviving (imposter, burnout) and choosing (IC track). Nothing about THRIVING — finding actual meaning in what you do.
**Best episode:** "How have I been complicit in creating the conditions I say I don't want?" — Jerry Colonna (podcast, 2025-05-08). Executive coach, former VC. The question that breaks people open. "The purpose of this question is to evoke your own agency." Also Chip Conley (podcast, 2025-08-03) — Brian Chesky's mentor. Employee pyramid: money → recognition → meaning. "Despair = suffering minus meaning." The more meaning, the less despair.
**Song potential:** "How have I been complicit?" — the most powerful coaching question in the archive. The meaning pyramid. Incredibly emotional, perfect for the Human Side album. Could make listeners cry.

### Gap 4: Storytelling / Communication
**Why it's missing:** The human side of work isn't just about feelings — it's about being heard. Communication is the meta-skill.
**Best episode:** "How to tell better stories" — Matthew Dicks (podcast, 2023-12-15). Author of Storyworthy. Every story is about a 5-second moment of transformation or realization. "I used to think X, now I know Y." Vulnerability is key. He helps companies and individuals tell their stories. Specific techniques anyone can use immediately.
**Song potential:** "Every story is about 5 seconds." The moment everything changed. A meta-song about the power of narrative — which IS what we're doing with these songs. Very meta, very Lenny.
