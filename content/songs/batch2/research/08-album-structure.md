# Batch 2 Album Structure

> Batch 1 organized songs by JTBD (Founders / Product / Builders / Human Side). Batch 2 introduces a new concept: **article-driven albums** where each album condenses Lenny's most notable long-form frameworks into catchy, memorable songs. These albums prove deep engagement with Lenny's content — turning 5,000-word articles into 3-minute anthems.

---

## Album 1: "The Marketplace Mixtape"

**Subtitle:** _How to build, grow, and win two-sided platforms_

**Why this album:** Lenny's marketplace content is legendary — the Facebook Marketplace inside story, the fundraising playbooks that fuel marketplace startups, and the PLG-to-sales evolution that every marketplace eventually faces. This album tells the story of building something where you need BOTH sides to show up.

### Source Articles
1. **The Inside Story of Facebook Marketplace** — Deb Liu (1B+ MAU, "five-year overnight success")
2. **GTM Motions of 30 B2B SaaS Companies** — 100% of PLG companies add sales
3. **How Today's Fastest-Growing B2B Startups Turned Early Users into Paying Customers** — 7/25 launched free
4. **Five Steps to Starting Your Product-Led Growth Motion** — Hila Qu (multiple aha moments)

### Track List
| # | Song Title Idea | Source Article | Hook |
|---|----------------|---------------|------|
| 1 | "Pave the Cowpath" | Facebook Marketplace | Find existing behavior, don't create new — 1B users by paving what Groups already did |
| 2 | "Both Sides Now" | GTM Motions of 30 B2B | Every PLG company adds sales — 100% of them. You can't escape it. |
| 3 | "Free First, Money Later" | Early Users → Paying | 7 out of 25 fastest-growing startups launched with no paid plan at all |
| 4 | "Multiple Aha Moments" | PLG Motion | PLG isn't one activation — it's user, team, buyer, and paid-customer level |

**Why Lenny would be proud:** This album synthesizes his most data-dense articles into sticky frameworks. "100% of PLG companies add sales" is the kind of counterintuitive stat that becomes a chorus. The Facebook Marketplace story anchors it with narrative weight.

## Album 2: "The Price Is Wrong"

**Subtitle:** _Everything founders get wrong about money_

**Why this album:** Pricing is the most under-discussed, highest-leverage topic in startups. Lenny's archive has a goldmine: SaaS pricing, AI monetization, freemium traps, the "double your price" mantra, and the Equals cautionary tale. This album makes pricing visceral and memorable.

### Source Articles
1. **Pricing Your SaaS Product** — Patrick Campbell / ProfitWell (30% revenue lift from localization)
2. **How Should You Monetize Your AI Features?** — 44 companies analyzed (59% bundle)
3. **Freemium vs. Trial** — 90% of freemium products also offer a trial
4. **Scaling Your B2B Growth Engine** — "Double your price until it stops working"

### Track List
| # | Song Title Idea | Source Article | Hook |
|---|----------------|---------------|------|
| 1 | "Double Your Price" | B2B Growth Engine | Segment's advice: keep doubling until customers push back. Most founders chronically undercharge. |
| 2 | "Freemium Killed Us" | Freemium vs. Trial + Equals story (from batch 2 candidates) | Free isn't a pricing strategy — it's an acquisition strategy. Equals learned the hard way. |
| 3 | "Bundle It In" | AI Monetization | 59% of companies bundle AI into existing plans. The market already decided. |
| 4 | "Every Quarter" | SaaS Pricing | Most successful companies optimize pricing every quarter. Everyone else sets it once and forgets. |

**Why Lenny would be proud:** This album turns his most actionable, numbers-heavy articles into earworms. "Double your price" is already a mantra — now it's a chorus. The Equals "freemium killed us" story (batch 2 candidate #5) bridges the article research and the podcast candidates perfectly.

## Album 3: "The AI Sessions"

**Subtitle:** _How to build, price, and ship AI products without losing your mind_

**Why this album:** AI is Lenny's hottest topic right now. His archive captures the lessons that are emerging in real-time: demo value vs. user value, how to price AI, how Perplexity builds with 2 PMs for 50 people, and the Intercom wartime story of pivoting to AI. This album is timely, provocative, and deeply Lenny.

### Source Articles
1. **Counterintuitive Advice for Building AI Products** — 20+ builders (demo value ≠ user value)
2. **How Should You Monetize Your AI Features?** — 44 companies (59% bundle)
3. **How Perplexity Builds Product** — 2 PMs for 50 people, slime mold org

### Track List
| # | Song Title Idea | Source Article + Podcast | Hook |
|---|----------------|------------------------|------|
| 1 | "Demo Magic" | AI Products article | What wows in a demo disappoints in daily use. GitHub Copilot: 35% acceptance rate. |
| 2 | "Wartime" | Intercom / Eoghan McCabe (batch 2 candidate #1) | $0 net new ARR → AI pivot → $1M to $100M ARR target. Six weeks to build a hero. |
| 3 | "Slime Mold" | How Perplexity Builds | 2 PMs for 50 people. 75% of goals are weekly. Organize like slime mold. |
| 4 | "Bundle It In (Reprise)" | AI Monetization | Callback to The Price Is Wrong — same framework, AI-specific lens. Or standalone. |

**Why Lenny would be proud:** This album captures the AI moment through Lenny's lens — not hype, but operator lessons. The Perplexity article and the Intercom podcast together show both the org model and the survival story of the AI transition. "Demo value isn't user value" is the kind of contrarian insight that defines Lenny's brand.

## Album 4: "First Hundred Days"

**Subtitle:** _The make-or-break moves every startup gets one shot at_

**Why this album:** Lenny's archive is uniquely strong on the early-stage playbook: fundraising, hiring, activation, finding your ICP. These aren't abstract — they're the specific moves founders make in their first hundred days that determine everything. This album condenses the survival guide.

### Source Articles
1. **A Playbook for Fundraising** — Marc McCabe (50-60 fund list, 10-week sprint)
2. **Raising a Seed Round 101** — Terrence Rohan & Jack Altman ($2-4M, 15% dilution)
3. **Winning at Early-Stage Hiring** — ~100 hours per hire, first 10 define culture
4. **What Is a Good Activation Rate?** — 34% average, 25% median across 500+ products
5. **How to Identify Your Ideal Customer Profile** — Start ultra-narrow, outbound data is #1 signal

### Track List
| # | Song Title Idea | Source Article | Hook |
|---|----------------|---------------|------|
| 1 | "Fifty Funds" | Fundraising Playbook | Build a list of 50-60 funds, compress outreach into 2-3 weeks, create FOMO. Fundraising is a sprint. |
| 2 | "A Hundred Hours" | Early-Stage Hiring | 100 hours per hire. First 10 hires define culture. Hiring IS fundraising in reverse. |
| 3 | "Twenty-Five Percent" | Activation Rate | 3 out of 4 signups never experience your value. Median activation is 25%. Fix this before acquiring more users. |
| 4 | "The Dumbest Idea" | ICP + Gamma podcast (batch 2 candidate #6) | Start ultra-narrow. The investor said "worst idea ever." $100M ARR later — narrowing down IS the strategy. |

**Why Lenny would be proud:** This album is the "Lenny starter kit" in song form. Every early-stage founder needs these frameworks. The numbers are so specific they become hooks: 50 funds, 100 hours, 25% activation, $2-4M seed. These songs would literally teach founders the playbook while they listen.
