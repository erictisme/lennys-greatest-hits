## Story Arc

Lenny surveyed current and past employees at **over 40 of today's most successful growth-stage companies** to answer one of the most common questions in product: "What should our North Star Metric be?" The result was a guest post on a16z's Future — his #1 most-viewed post of 2021 and a Top 15 all-time piece. The core thesis is deceptively simple: **"Your North Star Metric IS your strategy, and your strategy IS your North Star Metric."**

The drama comes from the consequences of getting it wrong — or getting it right. When Facebook changed its North Star Metric from monthly active users to daily active users, "the team suddenly had a lot more incentive to think about how to bring people back every day." That single metric shift made the product dramatically more engaging — so much so that Facebook ended up facing congressional scrutiny for being *too* addictive. The metric didn't just measure behavior; it *drove* behavior. What gets measured gets managed — and what gets managed transforms everything.

Sean Ellis — the original growth hacker, inventor of the Sean Ellis PMF test, former head of growth at Dropbox and Eventbrite — drives the point home on Lenny's podcast: a good North Star Metric should reflect the *value you deliver to customers*, not revenue. Amazon's isn't GMV — it's monthly purchases. Airbnb's wasn't booking revenue — it was nights booked. The metric should go "up and to the right over time," correlate with revenue but not *be* revenue, and a team should be able to pick one in 30 minutes if they have the right ingredients and framework.

## Emotional Core

This is about **clarity in chaos**. Every team, every company, every person faces the paralysis of too many metrics, too many goals, too many dashboards. The North Star Metric is the antidote — one number that tells you if you're winning or losing. It's the feeling of standing in a dark forest and finally seeing one fixed point in the sky. You stop spinning. You start walking. The emotional tension is between the overwhelming complexity of running a business and the radical simplicity of saying "THIS is the one thing that matters." There's also a darker undercurrent: choose the wrong star and you walk off a cliff (Facebook's addictiveness). Choose the right one and everything else falls into place. It's a high-stakes decision disguised as a simple question.

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches

**Pick one metric that measures the value you deliver to customers, and let it guide every decision — because whatever you choose to measure will shape everything you build.**
