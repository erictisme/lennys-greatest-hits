## Story Arc

Duolingo was already the world's most-downloaded education app when Jorge Mazal joined as Head of Product in late 2017, but growth had flatlined. DAU was growing single digits year-over-year. The company tried two big bets that both failed: copying a Gardenscapes "moves counter" mechanic (completely neutral result, team disbanded), and building an Uber-style referral program (only 3% lift). Jorge was deflated. The greatest effect was learning what NOT to do — you can't blindly copy features from other products without adapting them to your context.

The turning point came when Jorge built a growth model inspired by Zynga and MyFitnessPal — segmenting users into buckets (new, current, reactivated, resurrected, at-risk, dormant) and running sensitivity analysis on which lever would move DAU the most. The answer was stunning: CURR (Current User Retention Rate) had 5x the impact of the next best metric, thanks to its compounding effect — current users who stay active return to the same bucket, creating a flywheel. This became their North Star.

Armed with this clarity, the team made three breakthrough bets: (1) Leaderboards — adapted from FarmVille 2 with league-style progression, automatically opted in, tripled highly-engaged learners and boosted learning time 17%. (2) Push notifications — optimized ruthlessly with one golden rule: "protect the channel" (learned from Groupon's cautionary tale of destroying their email channel through over-sending). (3) Streaks — the simplest and ultimately most powerful feature. An APM discovered users who hit 10-day streaks had dramatically lower churn. The streak-saver notification (alerting users at night they're about to lose their streak) was the first big win. Over four years, the team ran 600+ experiments on streaks alone. They moved from an XP-based system to "just do one lesson" (huge win — simpler is better). They added streak freezes (two freezes was optimal — three trained users to take too many days off). They discovered that changing a button from "Continue" to "Commit to my goal" was a massive win. They found that sending practice reminders 23.5 hours after the user's last session beat any user-set reminder time. The result: CURR improved 21% (reducing daily churn of best users by 40%), DAU grew 4.5x over four years, from 5M to nearly 30M. Users with 7+ day streaks went from a small share to over half of all DAU. Today, 9 million users have a year-plus streak. The feature arguably created billions of dollars in value, helping power Duolingo to a $14 billion market cap.

## Emotional Core

This is a story about the magic of small, compounding commitments — the same psychology that makes habits, relationships, and careers work. The streak is a mirror for human nature: we don't want to break something we've built, day by day. There's pride in consistency ("I have a 1,435-day streak — with no streak freezes!"), anxiety about losing progress (people doing Duolingo at 11:45 PM at concerts), and the quiet satisfaction of showing up again. It's also an underdog comeback story — a mature product that everyone assumed had peaked, reignited by a team willing to fail twice, build a better model, and then obsessively optimize the simplest feature imaginable: a number that goes up by one every day. The tension between flexibility and perfection (the Streak Serenity Prayer) captures something universal: the balance between grace and discipline that makes any habit stick.

## The ONE Thing This Song Teaches

A tiny daily commitment, protected and celebrated, compounds into something massive — in habits, in products, and in life.
