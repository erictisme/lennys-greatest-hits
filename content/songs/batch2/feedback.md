# Batch 2 Feedback

25 songs generated. Research pipeline was excellent. Songwriting quality improved over Batch 1 but style choices were off.

## What Went Well
- **Two-phase pipeline** (research → songwriting) produced much richer source material than Batch 1
- **Research briefs** captured story arcs, quotable lines, and emotional cores effectively
- **Batch 1 mistakes** were largely fixed — less preachiness, more narrative arc
- **Killer lines** are genuinely quotable outside Lenny context
- **Song concepts** are strong — real protagonists, real stories, real tension

## What Went Wrong
- **Genre defaulted to aggressive rock** across most songs — Arctic Monkeys, Killers, Phoenix, Black Keys energy
- This is wildly off Eric's actual taste (Jon Guerra, Hollow Coves, CityAlight, Doris Club, Jacob Collier)
- **No critique/rewrite step** — lyrics went straight from first draft to final output
- **Style prompts were generic** — "anthemic rock 138 BPM" doesn't give Suno enough to work with
- **Some songs lack emotional progression** — they list ideas rather than building a narrative arc
- **BPM too high** on many tracks — 128-148 BPM when Eric's taste runs 72-120 BPM

## Rules for Next Batch
1. **Consult artist-palette.md** for every style choice. Default to acoustic/folk/neo-soul, not rock.
2. **BPM range 72-120** unless the content genuinely demands more energy. Even then, cap at 130.
3. **Add a critique step** (Step 6 in prompt-template.md) — read every line, flag blog-post language, rewrite.
4. **Add a rewrite step** (Step 7) — fix everything flagged in the critique before finalizing.
5. **Style prompts must be specific** — name reference artists from the palette, specific instruments, vocal character.
6. **Match genre to content energy** — a reflective song about burnout should not be garage rock at 148 BPM.
7. **Keep what worked** — the two-phase research→songwriting pipeline, the research brief format, the metadata.
8. **Test the universal angle** — would someone outside tech still feel something? If not, the images aren't strong enough.
