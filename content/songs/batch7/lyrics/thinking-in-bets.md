## Killer Line

> "There's no such thing as a long wait — you just decided not to look"

Placed as the opening couplet of every chorus. Set up in Verse 1 by showing someone rationalizing delay ("you tell yourself it's due diligence"). Lands hardest on second hearing once the bridge reveals the personal cost.

## Chord Progressions

**Verse: Ebmaj7 - Cm9 - Abmaj7 - Bb13**
Ebmaj7 gives the song its late-night luxe feel — warm, unresolved, expectant. Cm9 pulls inward. Abmaj7 is the breath of relief. Bb13 is the jazz-tension that never quite resolves — fitting for a song about decisions that stay open. This progression moves like a hand being considered at the table.

**Chorus: Fm7 - Bb7 - Ebmaj7 - Cm7**
Starts on the ii minor (Fm7) instead of the tonic — creates a feeling of suspension, like asking a question. Bb7 is the dominant — pulls toward Ebmaj7, which finally arrives with warmth. Cm7 at the end circles back to the beginning of the chorus for the repeat, never fully resting.

**Bridge: Abmaj7 - Gm7 - Fm9 - Bb13**
Starts on the IV (Abmaj7) for emotional lift — the "burning it down" moment. Gm7 adds shadow. Fm9 is the stripped, quiet moment of realizing what was already in your hands. Bb13 holds the tension before the final chorus release.
