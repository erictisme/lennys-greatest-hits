# Research Brief: Seventy-Five Businesses — Andrew Wilkinson / Tiny

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Andrew Wilkinson interview (July 3, 2025)
**Album:** The Interview

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Young barista, launches a web design agency (Metalab) almost by accident. Immediate win. Thinks he can do anything.

**Turning Point(s):**
- Loses money on a pizzeria, designer cat furniture, an online DJ school, skin cream, a news business. Then loses $10M competing with Asana via bootstrapped Flow.
- Reads about Buffett. Epiphany: stop starting, start buying. Builds Tiny into hundreds of millions.
- Hits $1B net worth. Still anxious, still miserable. Billionaire friends compare themselves to Bezos.
- Gets diagnosed with ADHD at ~40. Gets on SSRI + ADHD meds. Brain goes "from Times Square to a quiet library."

**End:** Runs Tiny (~$300M revenue, 40+ businesses including Letterboxd, AeroPress, Serato). Downsized lifestyle. Found more peace from a tiny yellow pill than all the money.

## Emotional Core

The lie that "more" will fix the feeling. More money, more companies — same anxiety. The antidote isn't hustle, it's strategy (fish where others don't) and chemistry (a tiny yellow pill). The shame of being anxious when you "have everything." Entrepreneurship as identity — "I always felt a bit broken."

---

## The ONE Thing

Stop fishing where everyone else is fishing. The biggest mistake is picking the exciting idea instead of the boring, profitable niche where no one is competing.
