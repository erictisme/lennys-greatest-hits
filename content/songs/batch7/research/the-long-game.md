# Research Brief: The Long Game — Nikhyl Singhal

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — "Building a long and meaningful career | Nikhyl Singhal (Meta, Google)" (June 11, 2023)
**Album:** Career Moves

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Nikhyl opens with a childhood image — growing up going to greyhound dog tracks. The mechanical rabbit is a metaphor for every career milestone people chase. Most people define their career around catching that one rabbit without thinking what happens next.

**Turning Point:** His own superpower (being decisively opinionated with little information) became his shadow. Someone he deeply trusted gave him feedback plainly. Rewiring took years.

**End:** Act three philosophy: for every Lenny who reinvented himself, there are hundreds who fall back into act two habits and get stuck. Start building your next North Star before you catch the current rabbit.

---

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- Mentored hundreds of product managers
- The Skip CPO community: started with 6 members, now 28
- Led product at Meta: groups, stories, messaging, the feed
- Background: Facebook/Meta, Google Hangouts, Google Photos, Credit Karma (CPO), 3 co-founded startups
- Team runs "version 7" of meeting operating system — redesigned every 90 days
- Argues tech professionals may work until 70s or 80s — careers 60 years long
- Lenny's career path: engineer → founder → PM → content creator (fourth career)

---

## Emotional Core

Quiet existential dread dressed as career advice. What if I catch the thing I've been running toward my whole life — and feel nothing? Ambition vs. meaning. The same drive that makes you successful hollows you out once you arrive.

---

## The ONE Thing

Stop optimizing for the rabbit. Start building a career that has reasons to keep running after you catch it.
