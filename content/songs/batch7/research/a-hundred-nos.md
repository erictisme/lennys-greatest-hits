# Research Brief: A Hundred Nos — Melanie Perkins / Canva

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Melanie Perkins interview (2025)
**Album:** The Startup Life

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** University student in Australia with no company, no code experience, no money, no connections. One vision: design will move online. Starts teaching design in her mom's living room, builds a yearbook tool called Fusion Books.

**Turning Point:** Pitches over 100 investors. Every single one says no. Each rejection becomes raw material — she adds a slide to answer each objection. The pitch deck sharpens with every no. Then one investor spends six hours on a call and gets it.

**End:** Canva reaches $42B valuation. $3.3B revenue. 240M monthly active users. Profitable for 8 consecutive years. She and co-founder Cliff pledge 30% equity to eliminate extreme poverty. $50M donated to GiveDirectly.

## Emotional Core

Quiet, stubborn belief. Not reckless optimism — certainty that the investors are looking at the wrong map. The gap between how small she was and how vast the vision was. The loneliness of being right before anyone believes you.

---

## The ONE Thing

Rejection is research. The dream doesn't care how many times you hear no.
