# Research Brief: Crossing the Chasm — Geoffrey Moore

**Source:** Lenny's Podcast — Geoffrey Moore interview (January 25, 2024)
**Album:** The Interview

---

## Story Arc

**Beginning:** Geoffrey Moore is at Regis McKenna, the premier high-tech marketing agency of the 1980s. They launch companies to wild success — Fortune covers, WSJ front pages. Then, two years later: "What happened to these guys?" Companies that looked like rockets just disappear.

**Turning Point:** The investigation reveals the pattern. Visionaries make buying decisions alone — they want to be different. Companies use visionary references to sell to pragmatists. But pragmatists think: "That's not my guy." The reference doesn't transfer. There's a gap with no bridge. The chasm.

**End:** 33 years later, over a million copies sold. Founders still say "our beachhead is the Fortune 500" and still run the match back and forth under the log.

---

## Specific Numbers & Facts

- Book published ~1991, over 1 million copies sold
- Beachhead goal: 30-40-50% market share in target segment within 2 years
- Target segment: top 20 customers
- Single-round-of-funding goal: cross the chasm within 18-24 months
- Documentum case: started in pharma (500K-page FDA docs; lost patent day = $1-2M/day)
- Marketing budget for beachhead: "maybe 200 people with a message"
- Crossing threshold: $10-20 million ARR, cashflow positive

## Emotional Core

The dread of the invisible trap. You're doing everything right — great product, visionary customers, press loves you. Then growth stops. It's a gap between two types of humans: those who run toward the future, and those who wait to see their peers move first.

**Human tension:** Every instinct says "go wide." The framework demands going narrow first. It feels like losing. It's the only way to win.

---

## The ONE Thing

You can't cross the gap by going wide. You cross it by going narrow first. Pick one pond. Hold the match. Light the kindling before you worry about the log.
