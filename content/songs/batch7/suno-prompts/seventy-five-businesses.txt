TITLE: Seventy-Five Businesses
STYLE: Lo-fi dream-pop, 4/4, 96 BPM, B major. Warm male vocal — conversational, wry, like a guy telling a story at a bar, not preaching. Finger snaps carry the rhythmic backbone throughout — intimate, human, no drum kit. Warm synth pad underneath, Rhodes or Wurlitzer flavor, low in the mix. Lo-fi vinyl crackle texture from the first bar. Upright bass, felt not heard, walking simply. Inspired by Doris Club and Tom Misch — groovy, sophisticated, light. No aggression, no intensity. The whole thing should feel like a friendly shrug. Synth pad swells slightly in the bridge as snaps drop out — one breath of space — then snaps return for the final chorus with a little warmth added. End on a single synth note fading into crackle. Chord movement: B - G#m - E - F# in verses, B - F#/A# - G#m - E in chorus (chromatic bass descent), Emaj7 - F#sus4 in bridge for slight harmonic color. Do not over-produce. Lo-fi warmth wins over crispness.
SOURCE: Lenny's Podcast — Andrew Wilkinson, founder of Tiny (75+ businesses). Failed ventures: coffee shop, cat furniture, DJ school, skin cream. Lost $10M bootstrapping Flow, competing with well-funded Asana. Charlie Munger "fish where the fish are" philosophy. Boring businesses (government form software: $30M/year) outperform exciting ones. Barista-to-billionaire arc. Still anxious despite the money. ADHD meds changed his life: "My brain went from Times Square to a quiet library." Met Steve Jobs at 17.
